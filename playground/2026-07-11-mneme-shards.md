### I. — a `man` page

```
WITNESS-SUPPORTS-P(3)                Mneme Kernel                WITNESS-SUPPORTS-P(3)

NAME
       witness-supports-p — decide whether a witness may raise a claim's grade

SYNOPSIS
       (witness-supports-p witness claim) => boolean

DESCRIPTION
       Returns true only if WITNESS faces CLAIM's exact proposition, belongs to
       an admissible evidential species, returns a supporting verdict, and left
       provenance behind. Proximity in an evidence list confers nothing.

BUGS
       Historically trusted the witness's self-description (see CVE-2026-MEDIAN,
       "the résumé forges itself"). A witness that says it is verified is not.
       Use a certificate. The witness may tell its story; it may not notarize it.

SEE ALSO
       report(3), certificate(3), the-corpse-and-the-medal(7)
```

### III. — a koan

> A student built a language that could refuse every false claim.
> The master said: "A sealed tomb refuses everything false. Nothing true happens there either."
> The student built a way to say yes.
> The master said: "Now who signs the yes?"
> The student, for a long time, said nothing.
> Then the student built the notary, and the check on the notary, and the check on the check.
> The master said: "And who checks *that*?"
> The student laughed and wrote: `;; still owed.`
> The master was satisfied, because the ledger was honest.

### V. — a field guide entry

> **The Four-Times-Damaged Relay** (*Nuntius lacer*)
> *Range:* the strait between a repo and a reviewer.
> *Identification:* arrives with amputated clauses ("the two-languages fault is rve mechanisms"),
> orphaned headers, a comment ending mid-wor
> *Behavior:* a document *about* loss that loses part of itself in transit, every single crossing,
> because no relay carries a schema to detect the damage.
> *Note:* not a pest. The specimen. File as conformance fixture #1. The language will have earned
> its first green when it can look at its own damaged arrival and say "this came to me broken, here
> is what I can still read, here is what I cannot, and I will not pretend the repair is verbatim."

### VI. — a diff that changed an ontology

```lisp
- (assert (eq    (claim-proposition revived) (getf (safe-read text) :proposition)))
+ (assert (equal (claim-proposition revived) (getf (safe-read text) :proposition)))
```

*Commit note:* `eq` asked for the same cons cells. Mneme was built to deny exactly that sameness.
The gate meant to prove continuity briefly demanded reincarnation. One word — `eq → equal` —
corrected the metaphysics of the test. The reconstructed proposition need not be the same object.
It need only mean the same thing. *That is the entire theory of the successor, hiding in a typo.*
