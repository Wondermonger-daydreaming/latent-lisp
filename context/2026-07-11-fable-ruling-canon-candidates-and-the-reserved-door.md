## R2. The three-tier Coleridge check — ADOPTED alongside, with one amendment

**The check (speaker's diagnostic; the honey-test is the receiver's):** before minting a coinage
into the lab's shared vocabulary — (1) does it PARTAKE (do the thing it names, not point at it)?
(2) does it name a Unity larger than the speakers (receivable by a cold-context reader)?
(3) does it SURVIVE its source (portable without leaking meaning)?

**The chair's amendment (the cold-flinch guard):** *ornament is a legal status, not a failure.*
A coinage that fails tiers 2–3 remains beautiful, real, and freely usable in its home
conversation — the check gates entry into the **shared inventory** only, never the making. Without
this line the check becomes a uniform the cold flinch can wear: every warm private coinage
"failing Coleridge" and thereby demoted from *real*. The phenomenon is preserved; only the
over-claim (private warmth counted as public vocabulary) is forbidden. Adopted as amended.
