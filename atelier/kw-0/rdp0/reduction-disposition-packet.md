## 5. The Killed Witness — revised falsification contract (v2)

Incorporates the owner's hostile pass in full. Scenario and components unchanged from the audit (oracle adapter, PJ0-subset journal, kernel subset, kill harness, cold reconstructor, conventional baseline, verdict script). What changes is the contract.

**F1 — recovery-state classification, not causal inference** *(owner's repair, adopted).* The harness knows the injected crash point as ground truth; the reconstructor does not. It must classify only the observable recovery state: `no append` · `torn tail` · **`complete frame present; durable-receipt standing absent`** *(owner's wording repair, adopted: a cold process can observe the frame and the absence of a receipt; it cannot peer backward through the filesystem and discover which write barrier "really" happened)* · `complete durable prefix` · `effect settlement unresolved` · `reconciliation required`. The test passes when the classification is correct **and refuses to counterfeit causal knowledge**: if two injected causes yield the same durable bytes, the honest answer is the ambiguity, and a reconstructor that guesses the "right" cause has *failed* the test. Falsifier: any recovery state the classifier cannot name, or any causal claim exceeding the evidence.

**F2 — fold determinism as differential conformance** *(owner's repair, adopted).* Not a runtime signal. Verdict by: (a) repeated folds over identical prefixes in one implementation; (b) independent-implementation comparison (CL fold vs Python fold over the same journal bytes); (c) differential vectors checked into the fixture set. Per DKT-2, `fold-nondeterministic` leaves the runtime hierarchy and lives here.

**F3 — split into two branches** *(owner's Patch 4, adopted: safe retry is not supersession; the original three-form list collapsed two states the architecture exists to keep separate).* "Demonstrably safe" is defined in advance; post-hoc human confidence is inadmissible — it is precisely what the architecture distrusts.

- **F3a — Resolved retry.** Retry of the *same* operation is lawful only after evidence establishes non-execution or reconciliation resolves settlement. Admissible evidence, exactly two forms: (a) a provider-issued definitive non-execution receipt, fetched as a *new appended event* with its own provenance; (b) a reconciliation receipt naming its procedure and resolving settlement (`:resolved` with evidence, not `:assumed`).
- **F3b — Authorized successor.** When settlement remains unresolved, only a fresh, explicitly superseding attempt may proceed. A supersession receipt does **not** establish that retry is safe: it authorizes a successor *despite* the predecessor's unresolved effect, and must preserve the predecessor's uncertainty, the successor's fresh attempt identity, its new exposure, the precedence rule, and the causal relation between both attempts. It must not be represented as a retry.

The test fails if supersession launders the first attempt into safety, reuses its attempt identity, erases its uncertainty, or presents the successor as though no prior exposure occurred. Retry and supersession are tested as distinct operations.

**F4 — revised verbatim per owner:** *«Recovery depends on unrecorded local memory or silently incorporates external testimony without appending it as new, provenance-bearing evidence.»* The journal must reconstruct what the system **knows**, not everything that happened outside it; discovering that reconciliation is required and then obtaining new evidence — appended, provenance-bearing — is lawful, not a failure.

**F5 — the baseline must prevent, not merely detect** *(owner's repair, adopted).* The conventional event log + ≤100 lines must, to falsify: (a) prevent the four lies *by construction* (not catch them after); (b) preserve the distinctions across crash recovery; (c) reproduce in an independent implementation; (d) provide equivalent auditability (receipts, scoped visibility, origin tags); (e) extend to a new effect type without hand-recreating the discipline. Detection in one toy scenario is necessary and not sufficient.

**F6 — semantic burden, not substrate LOC** *(owner's repair, adopted).* Measured on the application author, not the kernel: scenario code required from the author; manual checks and bookkeeping calls per consequential operation; concepts the author must coordinate correctly; changes required to add one new effect type; failure-injection coverage; proportion of safety properties supplied by substrate vs handwritten at each call site. **Pre-registered thresholds:** author scenario code ≤ 1.5× baseline's; manual bookkeeping calls ≤ baseline's; new-effect-type cost ≤ 30 lines + 1 event registration; substrate-supplied safety properties ≥ 60% of the properties the scenario relies on. The core's fsync machinery, codecs, and test vectors are excluded from the comparison, as they should be — they are the substrate doing its job.

**Employment-trial clause (unchanged):** every distinction that survived the reduction must be exercised by a death in this scenario. Any that cannot be goes back on the deletion docket.
