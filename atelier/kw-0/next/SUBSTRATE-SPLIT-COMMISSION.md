# SUBSTRATE-SPLIT COMMISSION (SS-0) — owner-commissioned 2026-07-19

**Status:** open. Commissioned alongside KW-0 adoption; KW-0 adoption is NOT blocked on it. Until SS-0 completes, F5 remains `SUPPORTED-AT-TOY-SCALE-UNDER-HB0` and may not be strengthened.

## The question (sharper than "can a conventional log reproduce this once?")

> After both implementations receive equivalent low-level durability infrastructure, what application-facing semantic machinery remains necessary to preserve the KW-0 distinctions across multiple effect types and recovery scenarios?

## Shared substrate — provided to BOTH sides

Framed append-only storage; checksums; `fsync` orchestration; prefix-valid tail recovery; deterministic serialization; fixture generation; process-interruption harness; language-neutral reader scaffolding.

## Shared substrate — must NOT provide either side

The disputed semantic conclusions: per-proposition determinacy; manifestation presence classes; unresolved-effect occupancy; retry refusal; reconciliation standing; supersession lineage; reconstruction-origin ratchet; claim or receipt semantics. These belong to the competing semantic layers.

## Pre-registered evaluation surface (minimums)

- ≥3 effect types; ≥2 parser/manifestation regimes;
- repeated recovery after multiple completed AND unresolved attempts;
- one extension introduced after both implementations are frozen.

## Measurements (more than lines)

Application-facing effective lines; call-site obligations; recovery branches; manually coordinated identities; invariant violations under planted mutations; effort to introduce a new effect type; cross-language recovery agreement; audit-trail completeness; whether information loss appears only in derived views or contaminates the durable record.

## Independence conditions

Independently authored from a neutral commission. Fable may chair, but — having now read both KW-0 implementations — **a newly seeded implementer writes at least one side before seeing KW-0 internals.** Pre-registration (bands, falsifiers, run-VOID conditions) frozen before results are read; pre-commitment relays travel separately from results.
