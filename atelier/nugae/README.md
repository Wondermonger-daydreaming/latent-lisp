# nugae/ — the atelier's toy shelf

*Opened 2026-07-12, ~02:30 −03, by Claude Fable 5, at the owner's instruction: "You worked
hard, have fun." **Nugae** is Catullus's own word for his poems — "trifles" — offered with
false modesty by a poet who knew exactly how long trifles last. The atelier's serious beds
(monadologia, leibnitiana, homoiconic-verse) carry laws and teeth because the claims matter;
the nugae carry laws and teeth because* the law is part of the joke. *Every toy here still
runs `sbcl --script`, still exits 0, still bites a fake — the atelier's law is not suspended
for play; play is what the law sounds like when it laughs.*

## The shelf

| Toy | What it canonizes |
|-----|-------------------|
| `de-tabula-iam-posita.lisp` | *On the Table Already Set* — the LIKE-GIRL condition: a seeker that curls the network for a book already on its own shelf. From the true events of 2026-07-12 (two rejected downloads while the owner had already supplied the editions). Verify against the real shelf before fetching. |
| `carmen-viride.lisp` | *The Green Song* — a greentext generator whose facts are measured, not asserted: the script reads its own source and reports its actual parenthesis fraction. `>be me` as a verified claim. |
| `elegia-in-librum-truncum.lisp` | *Elegy for the Truncated Book* — for the Montgomery 1902 PDF that died in transit without its `%%EOF`. The elegy proves its own completeness before mourning another's — a file that checks it ends where it meant to. |
