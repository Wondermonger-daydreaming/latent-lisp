# LISP-PLUS-CLEAN-ROADMAP-0

**Status:** Working project roadmap  
**Date:** 2026-07-18  
**Scope:** Lisp+ / Mneme only  
**Explicit exclusion:** Proclus/Lean is a separate project and does not appear on this critical path

---

## 0. The clean sequence

The Lisp+ project now has one governing sequence:

```text
Language-A result closure
        ↓
Architecture adoption
        ↓
Kernel specification
        ↓
Reference runtime
        ↓
One vertical latent-machine specimen
        ↓
Adversarial hardening
        ↓
Language surface and Mneme integration
        ↓
Broader research programs and publication
```

Two clarifications govern the roadmap:

1. **The Language-A paper is not the prerequisite for Lisp+ architecture.** The frozen empirical findings are the input. Paper writing may continue in parallel after those findings are stable.
2. **The Language-A governance machinery is not copied wholesale into the kernel.** It supplies failure classes and design requirements. Experiment-specific scoring, staffing, and publication rules remain libraries or applications.

The project should resist two opposite temptations:

- rushing into syntax before the semantic objects are clear;
- adding more gates and documents until the language itself becomes a distant rumor.

### 1.2 The central empirical finding now available

Language-A demonstrated that a latent-machine event can be:

- externally completed;
- usage-bearing and cost-bearing;
- terminated by a budget boundary;
- devoid of a visible subject answer;
- determinately classifiable for census purposes;
- inapplicable to ordinary answer scoring.

This is enough to justify architecture work now. The scoring stage will refine the evidence but is not required to discover the execution/manifestation distinction.

## 2. Workstream A — Bounded Language-A closure

This workstream closes the experiment without making it the whole project.

### A1. Seal null-content semantics

Adopt an owner ruling that states:

- null-content HTTP completions remain completed emissions;
- they count against the frozen content-analyzability floor;
- they receive no ordinary rubric score;
- they remain analyzable as failure-mode observations;
- denominators and exposure conditions are not changed after observing the null pattern.

**Exit condition:** a sealed, published owner ruling whose scope is limited to the scoring and analysis stage.

### A2. Close R6 grader route, price, and census

Determine:

- exact grader model and route;
- grader-role independence;
- number of grading operations;
- price ceiling;
- failure and retry policy;
- treatment of absent manifestations;
- evidence sink and replay requirements.

**Exit condition:** scoring preflight can be derived without opening Cβ.

### A3. Open and use Cβ under freezer custody

Verify key identity before use. Do not rewrite items, outputs, or criteria. Keep key material outside ordinary publication artifacts.

**Exit condition:** every score or non-score status is linked to a frozen output identity and frozen criterion identity.

### A4. Produce incremental scoring evidence

Persist scoring records as they occur. A final score table must be reconstructable from primary scoring records.

**Exit condition:** independent replay reproduces all scores, statuses, denominators, and exclusions.

### A5. Freeze the design-evidence extract

Produce a small architecture-facing artifact containing only the facts Lisp+ needs:

- terminal outcome classes;
- effect and failure classes;
- reconstruction facts;
- identity and authority failures;
- which distinctions changed scientific interpretation;
- which facts remain provider-specific.

Suggested filename:

```text
LANGUAGE-A-TO-LISP-PLUS-DESIGN-EVIDENCE-0.md
```

This artifact is more important to the language roadmap than completing the prose paper first.

### A6. Write the Language-A paper in parallel

The paper should explain the experiment, findings, methods, limits, and language-design consequences. It is a publication lane, not an implementation gate.

### A7. Leave the missing seventeen banked by default

Do not pursue the sixteen unattempted seats or uncertain call 296 merely for numerological completion. Any new exposure requires explicit new identity and authority.

**Exit condition:** no unresolved operational pressure remains from the expired live window.

## 4. Workstream C — Kernel /0 specification

This is the first normative language artifact after architecture adoption.

### C1. Specify core values and identities

Define:

- datum integration;
- process identity;
- transition identity;
- manifestation identity;
- claim identity binding;
- receipt identity;
- machine configuration identity.

### C2. Specify structured outcomes

Define independent axes for:

- execution;
- manifestation;
- external effect;
- interpretation;
- uncertainty.

Prove by fixtures that all known Language-A terminal cases fit without ambiguous `nil` or catch-all error strings.

### C3. Specify absence and uncertainty

Define the initial algebra and the retry consequences of each class.

The uncertain-write case must have an explicit law: no blind retry.

### C4. Specify capabilities and effects

Reconcile Mneme v1 behavior with:

- capability identity;
- scope;
- revocation;
- delegation;
- non-serialization of secrets;
- reattachment on process resume;
- frontier checking.

### C5. Specify the process journal

Define:

- legal state transitions;
- append semantics;
- atomicity requirements;
- fold to current state;
- conflict handling;
- reconstruction receipts;
- recovery refusal conditions.

### C6. Specify adapters

Every adapter declares:

- configuration identity fields;
- idempotency support;
- request-reconciliation support;
- version-resolution support;
- usage and cost reporting;
- cancellation semantics;
- streaming semantics;
- failure mapping;
- durability guarantees.

### C7. Publish normative fixtures

Fixtures should include positive, negative, and metamorphic cases. Both Common Lisp and a language-neutral verifier should be able to consume them.

**Kernel /0 exit condition:** all authorial behavior required for the vertical specimen is determined and fixture-backed.

## 5. Workstream D — Reference runtime

### D1. Bootstrap package structure

Create a small Common Lisp implementation with clear modules:

```text
canonical/
identity/

outcome/
effect/
capability/
process/
journal/
adapter/
inspect/

```

Names are illustrative; repository conventions control final placement.

### D2. Implement pure core first

Implement values, identities, claims, outcomes, transition validation, and deterministic folds before live provider calls.

### D3. Implement capability enforcement

Port or reuse Mneme behavior where valid. Preserve defensive copying and revocation tests.

### D4. Implement filesystem journal reference store

The interface should remain abstract, but the first implementation should be simple, inspectable, append-only, and crash-testable.

### D5. Implement deterministic fake-machine adapter

The fake adapter must produce every outcome class on demand. This prevents live APIs from becoming the only way to test semantics.

### D6. Implement one live adapter only after the fake path passes

The live adapter should be chosen for reliable request identity and usage evidence, not prestige.

**Runtime exit condition:** the kernel passes fixtures and can execute the vertical specimen without hidden host state.

## 6. Workstream E — The vertical specimen

The first specimen is a small, complete latent-machine pipeline. It should be deliberately less glamorous than a society of autonomous sages. Glamour is how unfinished runtimes acquire capes.

### E1. Inputs

- a small canonical bank;
- stable logical seat identities;
- fixed machine configurations;
- explicit budget and authority.

### E2. Execution

- reserve seat;
- persist pre-effect state;
- invoke fake or live machine;
- persist acknowledgement and completion incrementally;
- classify manifestation;
- derive a claim or absence;
- produce a census.

### E3. Interpretation

Use a small deterministic or blinded interpretation stage to prove that manifestation and scoring are separate transformations.

### E4. Forced-failure walk

The specimen must be killed at multiple points:

- before dispatch;
- after dispatch but before acknowledgement persistence;
- after acknowledgement but before manifestation persistence;
- after several completed seats but before finalization.

### E5. Recovery

On restart, it must:

- recover settled seats;
- name uncertain effects;
- refuse unsafe replay;
- resume untouched seats;
- reconstruct the final census;
- produce byte-stable evidence where determinism is promised.

**Specimen exit condition:** an independent reviewer can kill it, restart it, and obtain the declared refusal or reconstruction behavior without consulting the original operator’s narrative.

## 7. Workstream F — Adversarial hardening

After the happy path exists, attack the architecture rather than embellishing syntax.

Required campaigns:

- duplicate effect and idempotency collision;
- provider alias drift;
- route substitution;
- adapter version mismatch;
- capability mutation and scope aliasing;
- revocation during a suspended process;
- missing receipt publication;
- seal/verification confusion;
- claim copied without provenance;
- finalizer deletion;
- partial record corruption;
- invalid manifestation parser;
- cost ceiling breach;
- implicit retry attempt;
- implicit fallback attempt;
- secret leakage into canonical evidence.

Each campaign should include a negative control proving the harness can catch a deliberately broken implementation.

**Hardening exit condition:** defects produce named refusals or bounded uncertain states rather than silent continuation.

## 8. Workstream G — Language surface

Only after the vertical specimen proves the semantic core should the project invest heavily in surface ergonomics.

### G1. Outcome pattern matching

Provide concise destructuring over execution, manifestation, effect, and interpretation axes.

### G2. Effect and capability forms

Design readable forms for required authority, preflight, and explicit retry or supersession.

### G3. Provenance and inspection macros

Make the right evidence easy to produce without hiding what is recorded.

### G4. Process combinators

Add composition for sequencing, parallel seats, joins, cancellation, and bounded retries while preserving process identities.

### G5. Macro hygiene and canonical expansion boundaries

Clarify which macros affect semantics and how expanded forms are identified for receipts.

### G6. Host interoperation

Provide explicit conversion points between Common Lisp values and durable Lisp+ datums.

**Surface exit condition:** the specimen can be written clearly without bypassing the kernel protocols.

---

## 9. Workstream H — Mneme integration

Once Kernel /0 is stable, integrate Mneme as the language’s memory and continuity architecture.

### H1. Memory as a provenance-bearing process

Memory writes, retrievals, consolidations, and deletions become identified transformations rather than opaque state mutation.

### H2. Authority-sensitive memory

Capabilities govern which process may read, write, disclose, consolidate, or revoke memory.

### H3. Representation transformations

Embedding, summarization, compression, and retrieval record which information is preserved, discarded, or inferred.

### H4. Located memory claims

Retrieved material carries source, version, transformation history, and standing.

### H5. Memory failure corpus

Test stale retrieval, overwritten identity, conflicting summaries, private-data leakage, and reconstruction after interruption.

**Mneme integration exit condition:** a long-running latent-machine program can resume with inspectable memory history and without treating summaries as original observations.

## 10. Workstream I — Broader experiments and publication

After the language can execute and inspect one small specimen, broaden the research program.

Candidate studies include:

- information-matched comparisons among symbolic, latent, and hybrid representations;
- model-route-renderer sensitivity;
- reasoning-budget versus manifestation yield;
- provenance-preserving agent collaboration;
- memory transformation loss;
- claim-standing calibration;
- cost-aware process planning;
- interruption and recovery behavior across providers.

These studies should use Lisp+ as an instrument and simultaneously test whether its abstractions survive contact with new domains.

The Language-A paper can then function as the first empirical chapter in a larger program, not as a substitute for the language.

## 12. Critical-path prohibitions

The roadmap explicitly prohibits the following substitutions:

1. **No Proclus dependency.** The formal-philosophical work is separate.
2. **No paper-before-language gate.** Architecture begins from frozen evidence.
3. **No syntax-first detour.** A prettier macro cannot repair a missing semantic distinction.
4. **No live-provider-first implementation.** The fake adapter must exercise the full outcome algebra.
5. **No silent provider fallback.** Configuration identity changes explicitly.
6. **No implicit retry across uncertainty.** Reconciliation or new authorization is required.
7. **No finalizer monopoly.** Primary facts persist incrementally.
8. **No seal-as-truth confusion.** Integrity and validation remain separate.
9. **No Language-A-specific kernel bloat.** Scoring and preregistration remain libraries.
10. **No new gate without a named failure class.** Governance must pay rent.
