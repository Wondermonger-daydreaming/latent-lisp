## 3. REQUIRED REPAIRS

Ordered by depth. R1–R4 are algebra; R5–R7 are missing structure; R8–R9 are law-tightening and
de-moustaching. Where wording suffices, replacement text is given and marked **[TEXT]**. Where
behavior is authorially undetermined, it is named and sent to the docket, not invented.

### R1 — The absence family conflates STATE with CAUSE (§5.7)

The nine listed reasons mix census-facing *states* (`:completed-no-manifestation`, `:redacted`,
`:not-applicable`) with causal *diagnoses* (`:budget-exhausted-before-manifestation`) and
process-history facts (`:never-attempted`, `:pre-effect-refusal`). This is the "one theatrical
status enum" defect (§5.2's own words) recurring one section later. A diagnosis is a claim (lab
law: *a failure diagnosis is a claim*) and must be able to carry evidence and be wrong; a state
must be exhaustively enumerable for census folds. One enum cannot do both jobs — and the kimi
nulls prove it: their *state* (`no subject manifestation`) was determinate on the night; their
*cause* (`length-terminated reasoning exhaustion under the 768 cap`) is an interpretation that
could in principle be revised without changing a single census denominator.

**[TEXT] Replace the §5.7 list with:**

> **Absence** is a two-level family.
>
> The **state level** is closed per kernel version and census-safe:
> `:never-attempted` · `:refused-pre-effect` · `:absent-after-completion` · `:withheld` ·
> `:redacted` · `:not-applicable`.
>
> The **cause level** is an open, evidence-bearing vocabulary attached to a state:
> `(:absent-after-completion :cause :budget-exhausted :evidence usage-record)`. A cause is a
> claim: it carries provenance, may be `:cause :unestablished`, and may be revised by a later
> transformation without altering the state or any fold over states.
>
> "No answer appeared" and "no request occurred" are different **states**. "The budget ran out"
> is a **cause**, and writing it without evidence is writing an unverified diagnosis.

Consequence: `:budget-exhausted-before-manifestation` leaves the kernel enum (it is a cause, and a
provider-domain one); `:manifestation-empty` and `:manifestation-invalid` leave the family
entirely (R2).

### R2 — Empty and invalid are not absences: bytes exist (§5.5 / §5.7)

`:manifestation-invalid` sits in the absence family while "invalid manifestation" sits in the
manifestation-kind list (§5.5). It cannot be both, and it is neither: an invalid manifestation is
**present** — the payload exists, must be preserved as evidence, and may be re-parsed under a
later declared parser. Filing it as absence licenses discarding the very bytes a dispute would
need. Same for empty: an empty-string content field is an event with a payload of length zero,
observed at the interface — distinct from a response with no content field, distinct from no
response.

**[TEXT] Manifestation status becomes the kernel enum:**

> `:present` · `:present-empty` · `:present-invalid` (invalid **under a named parser identity** —
> validity is parser-relative, never intrinsic) · `:absent` (state per R1) · `:withheld` ·
> `:redacted`.
>
> Invariant: every `:present*` status preserves payload identity. An absence never carries a
> payload; if bytes arrived, the status is a `:present*` status, whatever the bytes' quality.

Authorially undetermined, sent to docket (**DK-2**): whether the kimi-null convention (empty
content field ≡ the banked "null content" census class) maps to `:present-empty` or
`:absent-after-completion`. Both are representable; the census fold needs to know which one the
frozen Language-A records mean. This is a reading of frozen evidence, so it is the owner's, and it
should be settled in the same breath as the A1 null-semantics ruling — **it is the same question
wearing kernel clothes.**

### R3 — Uncertainty is a MODE of each axis, not a sixth co-equal axis (§5.6)

The draft (and the packet summary) makes the outcome "a product of execution, manifestation,
effect, interpretation, **and uncertainty**." But uncertainty is not parallel to the other four —
it is a determinacy qualifier each of them can independently have. Call-296 shows it: execution
**indeterminate**, effects **uncertain**, manifestation **absent-so-far-as-evidence-shows**,
interpretation **not-applicable**. A single outcome-level uncertainty slot invites exactly the
regression the claim facets were built against: one pompous number ("this outcome is 80%
certain") smearing over *which axis* is unknown — and it double-books with the uncertain-effect
record, which already carries effect-axis uncertainty with structure.

**[TEXT] Replace the §5.6 axis list with:**

> An **outcome** is a product of four axes — execution, manifestation, external effect,
> interpretation — each of which carries its own determinacy: `:determinate`, `:bounded`
> (alternatives named, evidence attached), or `:indeterminate`. There is no outcome-level
> uncertainty scalar. The uncertain-effect record (§5.8) is the structured form of a `:bounded`
> or `:indeterminate` effect axis; usage/cost observations, process identity, receipts, and
> bounded unknowns ride the outcome as before.

This makes the outcome algebra strictly smaller and closes a class of impossible states (globally
"determinate" outcomes containing an uncertain effect).

### R4 — The execution axis has no defined values (§5.6)

The draft enumerates process states (§5.4) and absence reasons (§5.7) but never the execution
axis itself; its worked examples only ever show `:completed`. The nine terminal cases cannot be
checked against an undefined axis. Proposed kernel values, checked against all nine cases in
Appendix B: `:not-attempted` · `:refused` (pre-frontier by definition) · `:failed` (with
`:pre-frontier` / `:post-frontier` qualifier) · `:completed` · `:indeterminate`. A "refusal"
after the frontier is not a refusal; it is a failure or a cancellation — the draft's own §5.4
distinction, which the outcome axis must inherit.

With R3+R4, Appendix B shows all nine Language-A terminal cases land without ad hoc fields, catch-all
strings, or `nil` — and case 9 (reconstructed census) lands **outside** the outcome algebra on
purpose: a census is a fold-derived *claim* (origin `:reconstructed`), not the outcome of any call.
The charge asked the product to represent it; the honest answer is that it must **refuse** to — an
algebra that could swallow a census as an outcome would be re-conflating the claim plane with the
manifestation plane.

### R5 — MISSING KERNEL PRIMITIVE: attempt identity (the logical seat) and supersession

The draft's most load-bearing undefined term. §6.2 refuses dispatch when "an unresolved prior
effect occupies the same **logical seat** or idempotency domain"; §7.3 permits retry via "a new
**logical attempt identity** and an explicit **supersession relation**"; §15.6–8 test duplicate
seats, duplicate idempotency identities, and occupied targets; the call-296 protocol (already
adopted lab-side) requires "new exposure identity + supersession relation + declared precedence
rule." None of these can be stated without a first-class notion of *the same work*: an identity
for the logical operation that is stable across processes, attempts, and reconstruction, plus a
relation by which one attempt lawfully replaces another.

**Add to §5 as a core entity:** attempt identity (seat), bound to: logical-operation identity,
scope (which bank/domain it belongs to), occupancy state (derived from the journal, never stored
as a mutable flag), predecessor attempts, supersession records (each naming authorization and
precedence rule). Kernel primitive — it is the thing idempotency, no-double-exposure, and lawful
retry are *about*, and no library can retrofit it.

### R6 — Visibility is relational; the facet must carry scope (§5.2)

`:published` — where? `:withheld` — from whom? The lab's own practice refutes intrinsic
visibility: the leibnitiana cold-read packet is *withheld from the public mirror* and
*published to its recipient*, simultaneously and deliberately. A single-valued facet cannot say
this, and a facet that cannot say it will be gamed by the first workflow that needs "published"
to be true in one room and false in another.

**[TEXT] Amend the visibility facet:**

> Visibility values name their scope, as validation names its validator: `(:published :scope
> mirror)`, `(:withheld :scope public :basis capability-id)`. A claim may carry multiple
> visibility records with disjoint scopes. Bare `:published` is as oversized as bare
> `:verified`.

Also (wording only): `:withheld`/`:redacted` now appear in three places — claim visibility,
manifestation status, absence states. Same words, three planes. Add one disambiguation sentence
so the robes stay on their owners.

### R7 — The ruling→capability bridge: capability minting provenance (§5.9)

"Authority is not capability merely asserted in prose" — yet every real authorization in this
project *begins* as prose: a sealed owner ruling. As drafted, the architecture's own adoption
process (owner rulings, freezer custody, R13→R15-style windows) sits outside its authority plane,
which would make the lab's actual governance unrepresentable in the lab's own language. The
separation is correct; what is missing is the lawful crossing: **minting**. A capability comes
into being by an act that must itself carry provenance — minter identity, the authorizing claim
(the sealed ruling, by claim identity), scope derivation, expiry/revocation registry.

**Add to §5.9:** a capability records its minting receipt: `(minted-by authority-id :under
authorizing-claim-id :scope ... :registry revocation-registry-id)`. Prose does not become
authority; a *sealed, identified* prose ruling is the evidence under which authority is minted,
and the mint is inspectable like any transformation. (This also resolves the §8.4 tension: resume
checks the *requirement + registry*, since the live authority was never serialized — the durable
half of the capability is precisely the minting receipt.)

### R8 — Journal laws must not promise more than the filesystem gives (§8.1, §8.4)

Three annotations, each from a lived failure class:

1. **Torn tails.** "Kill at any instant" includes mid-append. Define the fold over the
   longest *prefix-valid* journal; a torn trailing record is reported as evidence, never
   silently dropped, never fatal to the settled prefix. (§15.16 tests corruption; the law
   should state the recovery semantics the test checks.)
2. **Durability is declared, not assumed.** fsync semantics vary by host and filesystem; the
   store protocol (D4) must have each store *declare* its guarantee (synced / best-effort),
   and reconstruction must treat best-effort tails as `:bounded`. A law that says "lands
   durably" without naming the mechanism is a prose guarantee — the weakest kind.
3. **One journal, one order; merges are transformations.** The deterministic fold requires a
   total order, which exists only within a single append-only journal. Cross-journal
   composition (parallel shards — the lab has done this twice) is a *reconstruction
   transformation* with declared ordering rules and a receipt, never an implicit
   interleaving. Wall-clock timestamps are observations riding the records, not the order.

### R9 — De-moustaching: three Language-A tokens leave the kernel

1. `:subject-exposure` and `:score-under-key` leave the representative effect-tag list (§7.1).
   Their generic classes already exist (epistemic effect; `:secret-open` per D8's draft
   preference). They re-enter as Language-A library refinements. Keeping them in the kernel's
   own example list is the moustache the relay warned about.
2. **"Census" is a library noun.** The kernel has *deterministic folds over journals*; an
   experiment library names one such fold a census. §8.1 and §11.1(10) should say "fold /
   derived view"; §14's specimen may say census, because the specimen is an application.
3. The absence-cause `:budget-exhausted-*` leaves the kernel enum via R1.

And one **anti-moustache confirmation**, because the hostile simplifier must also protect: typed
absence, uncertain effect, manifestation/execution split, and reconstruction standing are **not**
Language-A in disguise. Each is reachable from at least one non-LLM latent-machine case
(embedding retrieval returning nothing under a filter; a tool call that timed out after side
effects; a recurrent memory whose state advanced without an emission). They stay.

## Appendix A — The five separations, attacked (charge §2, §3)

**A/1 datum–claim:** Held. Sharpest repo probe: a digest inside `SHA256SUMS.txt` — is the datum an
assertion? No: the *file* is the claim (asserted, checkable), the digest is its content. The
separation is layering, not disjointness; claim content is itself datum (§5.1 already says this).
**A/2 execution–manifestation:** Held; the 76 kimi nulls are its existence proof. Annotation: a
provider refusal in text is a **present** manifestation whose interpretation is `:refused` — it
must not be typed as absence. (The night's other refusals — EMITTER's, EMITTER-III's — are
process-level `REFUSED`, a third thing. Three refusals, three planes, no shared enum.)
**A/3 manifestation–interpretation:** Held. A structured tool call *looks* pre-interpreted; it is
not — schema validity is parser-relative (R2) and semantic license is a further transformation.
**A/4 authority–assertion:** Held, with the missing bridge repaired by R7. The near-counterexample
is the lab itself: sealed prose rulings *are* operative authority here. Resolution: rulings are
authorizing claims under which capabilities are *minted*; the runtime authority is the
capability; the ruling is its provenance. Without R7 this separation would indict the project's
own governance; with R7 it describes it.
**A/5 completed–repeatable:** Held. Call-296 (uncertain, quarantined, never blindly re-fired) and
the option-(a) census idiom (80 determinate nothings, zero retries) are the two lived poles.

**Claim-facet orthogonality (charge §3):** Probed by hunting impossible pairs. `:asserted`+
`:verified` — legal (assertion later verified; origin is history, validation is afterward).
`:refuted`+`:sealed`+`:published` — legal and *necessary* (the erratum discipline: wrong claims
stay, marked). `:indeterminate`+`:verified` — legal only because validation carries scope (what
was verified is the bound). No facet entails another; the five answer five different questions.
Two repairs only: visibility scope (R6), and one sentence noting that **sealing attests the
canonical record embodying a claim, not the proposition** — standing travels from record to
proposition only through identity.
