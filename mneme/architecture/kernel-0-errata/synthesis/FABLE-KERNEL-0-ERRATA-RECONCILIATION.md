### 1.1 The load-bearing step: my KE-5 fails my own KE-2 standard

My KE-2 (singleton lawful) carried its own burden: *"a singleton `:bounded` record
asserts that its enumeration is exhaustive over the declared space; its evidence MUST
support the exhaustiveness of the elimination."* My KE-5 then derived call-296's
singleton by eliminating every `:present*` candidate on the ground that *no captured
envelope exists, so nothing can be projected* (DK-2/§8.8).

But that elimination is **not evidence-supported for an uncertain write — by the lab's
own crash-window law, which my own Gap-2 section imports.** Under PJ0 CW-1/CW-2 and
Kernel §13.8, the valid prefix's silence about a capture event is *not* evidence that no
capture occurred: a capture (or a chunk) could have been in flight and lost with the torn
tail or the unflushed buffer. The record's own execution-indeterminacy says precisely
that we do not know what happened at the boundary. So the elimination of
"present-but-lost" rests on absence-of-record in exactly the scenario where
absence-of-record is uninformative. The closed vocabulary cannot *name* the
present-but-lost alternative (§9.6: `:present*` requires a payload identity) — which
means the "exhaustive" singleton is exhaustive only over what the vocabulary can say,
not over what the evidence leaves open. **My KE-2's exhaustiveness burden is therefore
unmeetable for call-296, and my KE-5 construction fails under my own rule.** GPT's
concordance said this compactly: *"the completion-presupposition tension is evidence
that the closed vocabulary is missing a state, not evidence that the existing singleton
is complete."* That is correct.
