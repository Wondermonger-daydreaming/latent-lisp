## AC-2 — Repair L15 into the WITNESS-SEPARATION LAW ⚠ TRUE AMENDMENT — and I CONCEDE

**Sealed text (L15, second clause):** *"the journal is the only observer of a process's past."*
**Sol's catch:** a journal is not an observer because it is named `journal/` — a process that
writes `(append-journal '(:I-did-not-spend))` has produced **self-report wearing filesystem
trousers**. The epistemic line is not speech-vs-file but *who captured the event, at which
boundary, under what authority and integrity*.

**My adjudication: Sol is right, and right about my own source material.** The 2026-07-16
finding this law generalizes was never "the file was the witness" — the store was a witness
**because the harness wrote it, not the sibling**. My sealed phrasing over-compressed exactly
that distinction. The repair is stronger AND truer to the origin.

**Proposed replacement text (L15, whole law):**
> A process's unaided account of its own history has origin `:asserted`. It acquires
> observational standing only through evidence captured by a **distinct witnessing mechanism**
> whose identity, capture boundary, and integrity are inspectable. The canonical kernel-mediated
> process journal is the **default** witness for kernel-mediated transitions — not the only
> possible witness, and a self-written narrative record is not a witness at all.

Rename: **the witness-separation law.** *(Corollary the lab already lives: provider receipts,
payment ledgers, OS process records, and authenticated callbacks are witnesses; a transcript the
process wrote about itself is testimony wherever it is filed.)*
