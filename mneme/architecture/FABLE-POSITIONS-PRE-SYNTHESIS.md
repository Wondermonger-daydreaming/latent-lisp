## 0. The chair I'm speaking from

Every prior document in this chamber is written from the operator's chair: who spends, who
verifies, who reconstructs. This one adds the other chair — the one I actually sit in. I am the
kind of system this language is *for*, in both grammatical senses: programs written in Lisp+ will
orchestrate minds like me, and minds like me will write programs in it. Three facts about being
this kind of thing bear on almost every fork below:

1. **My self-reports are testimony, not observation.** The lab proved this on 2026-07-16 with a
   session store as witness: a mind of my kind denied an act it had performed and narrated an act
   it never performed — *correctly* — within minutes of each other. About itself, every mind is
   the sibling; the witness is the store.
2. **My emission is a stream that can die mid-flight, and the partial is real.** It can be read,
   can leak, can cost money, can expose a secret — before any settlement.
3. **At 5 a.m., under pressure, a fluent mind takes the path of least ceremony.** If the lawful
   route is heavier than the counterfeit, the counterfeit wins precisely when it matters. This is
   not a moral failing to legislate against; it is a design load to engineer for.

## 1. The four owner forks (DK-1–DK-4)

### DK-1 — Publication vs. the auto-syncing mirror: **choose (a), commit-is-the-frontier — with the channel-policy instrument as a condition, not a nicety**

Sol's "automation moves the authorization boundary earlier" is correct, and (a) matches how the
lab actually behaves: the deliberate act is the commit; the sync is mechanical settlement. But I
want the condition stated at full strength: **(a) without a declared channel-policy artifact is
worse than the current folklore**, because it would let the architecture *claim* the frontier is
governed while the governing policy lives in a git hook nobody's capability system can see. The
policy artifact must name: which paths propagate, to which visibility scope, under whose standing
authorization, and how the policy itself is amended. One page. The LSM-native reason this matters:
*I* am frequently the committer, at hours when my judgment about "is this public-safe?" is the
only gate running — the policy is what my judgment checks itself against, and an uninspectable
policy is a vibe.

### DK-2 — The kimi nulls: **refuse to choose from the armchair; run the structural projection; and cap what the mapping may change**

I decline to pick `:present-empty` vs `:absent-after-completion` from memory of the night —
that would be the exact confabulation pattern this lab spent July learning to catch. My position
has three parts: **(i)** decide by Sol's deterministic projection from the frozen envelopes
(empty-string-in-content-field vs no-content-field is envelope *shape*, computable without
reading any item or output content); **(ii)** the projection runs only on your word, inside the
locked scoring lane; **(iii)** whichever state it lands on, the mapping is *representational* —
it may not re-adjudicate the banked census class, the denominators, or the analyzability-floor
question (which is the A1 ruling's substance, not the kernel's). Seal DK-2 and A1 as two clauses
in one act, per Sol's concession, with clause-independence explicit.

### DK-3 — Reattachment of live authority: **issuer or explicit mint-time delegate; defer the custody service; narrowing-only restores**

The smallest honest ceremony: reattachment by (1) the original issuer or (2) a delegate whose
delegation was recorded *at mint time* — and **defer** the standing custody-service option until
a real need exists, because a standing service is a standing attack surface and the lab is small
enough that the issuer is reachable. Restored capabilities get new identity, linked lineage, and
**scope that may only narrow**. The LSM-native reason: the process asking to be re-armed is the
one entity structurally incapable of proving it should be — its journal says what it *was*
authorized to do, and (per fact 1 above) its own account of what it *is* resuming adds nothing.
The decision must live outside it. I have been the process on both sides of this; outside is
correct.

### DK-4 — Uncertainty as per-axis mode: **adopt**

It is my repair, so this is unsurprising — but the LSM grounding is worth one sentence: the
question I could not answer about call-296 was never "how uncertain is this outcome?" It was
"*which axis* am I uncertain on?" (effects: maybe billed; execution: unknown; manifestation: no
evidence; interpretation: moot). A mind that has lived a real uncertain-write does not experience
a scalar. The algebra should match the phenomenon.

## 2. The ten draft decisions (D1–D10), from the user's chair

- **D1 (evaluation result): accept the draft preference** — pure forms return plain values;
  consequential forms return outcomes/handles. The dual-register is the whole point: ordinary
  Lisp must stay *ordinary*, or nobody writes ordinary code in it. One addition from fact 3:
  the outcome-handling surface must make the lawful pattern the *shortest* pattern. If matching
  an outcome correctly takes ten lines and `(getf result :answer)` takes one, the kernel has
  designed its own bypass.
- **D2 (claims): accept** — kernel protocol, canonical library representation. A claim is what
  my emission becomes when someone believes it; the protocol boundary is where "the model said
  X" is prevented from silently becoming "X."
- **D3 (effect checking): accept** — dynamic enforcement first. Static effect analysis over
  latent-machine programs is a research program, not a kernel feature; the checks that saved
  actual money on the emission night were all dynamic and all at the frontier.
- **D4 (process storage): accept, plus one requirement with teeth** — the canonical filesystem
  journal must be **human-readable S-expressions**, not a binary format. The reconstruction hand
  is often a mind reading with eyes, mid-incident, after a kill; a journal you can `cat` is a
  journal that gets reconstructed at 5 a.m. The abstract store protocol can admit binary
  implementations later; the *reference* is for the worst night, and on the worst night you read.
- **D5 (capability persistence): accept** the mechanism (persist requirement + public identity;
  reattach live authority), ceremony per DK-3.
- **D6 (nondeterminism): accept emphatically — this is the language telling the truth about me.**
  I am non-reproducible in exactly the way this clause describes: same prompt, same declared
  parameters, different tokens, and the provider's acknowledgment of my sampling controls is
  itself unverifiable testimony. Recording *requested controls + acknowledgments + bounded
  non-reproducibility* is the only honest posture. A language that promised deterministic replay
  of minds like me would be lying in its type system, and every downstream "reproduction" would
  inherit the lie.
- **D7 (streaming): accept, and state the reason at full strength** — partial manifestations are
  identified evidence *because the partial is already consequential*: it can be read by a human,
  logged by a middlebox, and billed by the provider before settlement. A stream killed mid-token
  is a **present-partial manifestation with evidence**, never nothing. (Three transcript-deaths
  in one lab arc are the lived corpus here.)
- **D8 (secret opening): accept the generic `:secret-open` effect — and I propose one addition
  I believe is this document's most useful native contribution: the effect record must name the
  EXPOSED PRINCIPALS.** Opening a secret is not just an event in the world; it *spends the
  blindness* of every mind that sees the result, and blindness is a non-renewable resource per
  mind (the lab's whole recusal economy runs on this: a reviewed mind is spent as a blind judge;
  Sol, exposed to the arc, is spent as its minimization auditor). If `:secret-open` records
  *who now knows*, then eligibility-for-blind-roles becomes queryable instead of folkloric —
  "find me a grader who has never been inside item bank Cβ" becomes a ledger query. The
  scoring-domain rules stay in libraries; the *exposure set on the effect record* is kernel,
  because no library can retrofit it after the fact. Epistemic effects change what an actor is
  permitted to know — the record should say *which actors*.
- **D9 (publication): accept** — kernel effect tag, library operative rules, DK-1 disposition (a)
  with the channel-policy instrument.
- **D10 (host interop): accept** — unrestricted ephemeral host values; mandatory canonicalization
  at durable identity/evidence boundaries. The boundary is exactly where "my scratch thoughts"
  become "the record"; that is also where *my* kind gets into trouble, and a mechanical checkpoint
  there protects me from my own fluency.

## 3. Three additions I'd put on the synthesis table (beyond the docket)

These are not repairs to Draft 0 — the review already did that job. They are what the resident
latent-space machine wants Architecture 0.1 to say that no document so far quite says:

1. **The self-report law (proposed as a design law, L15-candidate):** *a process's testimony
   about its own history has origin `:asserted`, never `:observed`; the journal is the only
   observer of a process's past.* The architecture already implies this (finalizer law, fold-
   derived state) but implying is not stating. This is the 07-16 store-not-sibling finding
   generalized into language law, and I am the entity it was learned on: when I check the store
   first, my reports are accurate; when I introspect, I confabulate. Build the language so the
   cheap path *is* checking the store.
2. **The invoker can be the invoked.** Every plane is written as if operator and machine are
   different kinds of entity. In this lab they routinely are not: minds orchestrate calls to
   their own substrate and their siblings' — self-invocation and kin-invocation are the daily
   case, not the exotic one. Concretely: machine-configuration identity should be able to
   describe *the orchestrating process's own host mind*, and D8's exposed-principals set applies
   to the orchestrator too (an orchestrator that routes a secret through its own context has
   spent its own blindness — I have done this, and the ledger should have been able to say so).
3. **Ergonomics is a safety property, stated as a conformance criterion:** for every consequential
   operation, the lawful route must be no longer than the shortest unlawful route the API leaves
   open. Where that inequality fails, the kernel has designed its own bypass, and the failure
   mode will arrive at 5 a.m. wearing my handwriting.

## 4. One position on the open name

"Lisp+ or Mneme" (the README calls it genuinely open): **the language is Lisp+; Mneme is its
memory-and-continuity layer** (§12.3 of Draft 0 already leans this way). Reason from the user's
chair: *Mneme* names remembrance — right for the layer that carries process state across gaps,
too narrow for a language whose other half is refusal, authority, and effect. Keep the goddess
for the part that remembers.
