## My disposition on Opus's six remaining pressures

I would divide them into three classes.

### Already partly governed, but needing explicit freeze enforcement

**B — grader shared roots and source-packet access.** This is not entirely
absent. The original ruling already excludes any item seen by a grader
model during rubric calibration and requires read-event logging; the
erratum preserves that architecture. But Opus has found the operational
seam: rubric calibration could expose graders to the *shape* or source
ancestry of the held-out bank without literally showing them the final item
rendering.

The freeze packet should therefore rule:

* rubric calibration uses synthetic, permanently tainted examples only;
* no target-bank source packet, item text, trap class, keyed disposition,
  or paraphrase may be read during calibration;
* graders may receive the frozen source packet only when scoring the
  locked target response;
* all such reads are versioned events;
* a grader exposed early may still score exploratorily, but cannot belong
  to the blind primary grading panel.

That belongs in `FREEZE-STAFFING.md`, `KEY-CUSTODY-RECEIPT.md`, and the
lineage ledger—not in another program erratum.

**C — the SHAM sample.** Opus is right that 24 SHAM calls cannot support a
strong positive causal claim about ceremonial novelty versus semantic
structure. But the original ruling already calls SHAM a diagnostic subset
and says it cannot rescue the primary contrast. The missing piece is a
harder language ceiling:

> SHAM may classify uptake, disengagement, and detectable semantic leakage.
> It may not establish that ceremonial salience has been eliminated,
> isolated, or causally explained.

So SHAM remains useful, but it is a **manipulation diagnostic**, not a
miniature fifth efficacy arm pretending to have power it was never given.

### Genuine packet requirements still missing

**D and E — negative overclaim control plus an actual teeth-check.** Strong
agreement.

The claim ceiling currently prevents grand positive claims such as "Language
A makes models truthful," but negative claims can also escape their finite
cage:

```text
Language A does not help.
Language A fails.
The approach is ineffective.
The notation has no value.
```

None of those follows from `B-INCONCLUSIVE`; even `B-NULL` supports only
bounded practical equivalence under this finite design. `B-HARM` remains
localized to the named outcome and sample.

The packet should contain a claim linter—probably `harness/claim_lint.py`—
that checks every generated branch receipt, README conclusion, abstract,
and machine-generated summary. It should reject forbidden naked templates
and require the experiment's riders.

Not just `grep`, though grep is a fine first tooth. A robust linter should
enforce both:

```text
forbidden unbounded phrases
```

and:

```text
mandatory bounded-language elements
```

For example:

```text
under the frozen pilot
first-pass emission
sampled releases/routes
pilot scale
does not establish hidden reasoning
does not establish enforcement efficacy
```

Suggested local conditions:

```text
ForbiddenUnboundedClaim
MissingClaimCeilingRider
InconclusiveNarratedAsNull
LocalizedHarmOvergeneralized
ShamDiagnosticOverclaimed
```

That would make the claim ceiling executable rather than decorative
scripture.

**F — a fresh adjudicator.** The instinct is sound, but I would change
"fresh weights" to **fresh chair**.

Literal fresh weights may be unavailable, unverifiable, or conceptually
misleading. A different endpoint can still share training roots, evaluator
conventions, and provider infrastructure. What we can manufacture and
receipt is:

* a fresh session;
* no item or rubric authorship;
* no target-output access before adjudication;
* no participation in grader calibration;
* no score-key access beyond the exact adjudication slice;
* an independently logged access packet;
* preferably a different model family or human reviewer, where available.

If no such chair exists, adjudication can still proceed, but the receipt
must name the shared roots and cannot claim adjudicator independence. The
scientific requirement is not ritual purity; it is **no hidden reuse of
the same interpretive pathway while calling it a second judgment**.

### One actual authority-return edge

Opus's final point about structurally unreachable branches is the sharpest
new thing.

The erratum permits retaining the design as feasibility-oriented or
acknowledging that `B-INCONCLUSIVE` will dominate. But if the synthetic
study shows that **a substantive branch cannot fire even under a canonical
favorable scenario designed to instantiate its own truth**, then the branch
bank is not merely low-powered. It contains a painted door.

For example, if under synthetic near-zero heterogeneity, strong paired
correlation, and a true difference entirely inside `±0.05`, the `B-NULL`
interval still cannot fit inside the equivalence band, then the design
cannot distinguish practical equivalence from uncertainty. Retaining
`B-NULL` as a nominal outcome would be false affordance.

That should explicitly trigger:

```text
PILOT-AUTHORITY-RETURN — STRUCTURALLY UNREACHABLE BRANCH
```

before the real item bank is frozen.

I would not invent a generic power threshold here. Instead freeze a cleaner
criterion:

> Each substantive branch must fire in at least one predeclared canonical
> favorable synthetic scenario constructed to satisfy that branch's own
> predicate. If a branch fires in none of its favorable scenarios, and the
> failure is attributable to design geometry rather than simulation error,
> the packet must return for authorial ruling.

This separates:

* low but nonzero operating power, which may be accepted as a feasibility
  limitation;
* from **structural unreachability**, which is a malformed branch bank.

I would append this focused addendum to the Codex commission:

```text
ADDITIONAL FREEZE-PACKET OBLIGATIONS
LANGUAGE-A EMISSION PILOT

These requirements clarify packet construction under the controlling program
ruling and Errata 0.1. They do not authorize live exposure or alter the
experiment's frozen margins, arms, branch definitions, or claim ceiling.

1. GRADER CALIBRATION AND SOURCE-PACKET FIREBREAK

Rubric calibration must use synthetic, permanently tainted examples only.

Before locked target scoring, no primary grader may read or receive:

- a target-bank item;
- a target source packet;
- a paraphrase or rendering of a target item;
- its trap class;
- its scorable opportunities;
- its keyed disposition;
- or calibration material derived from the target bank.

A primary grader may read the exact frozen source packet only when scoring
the corresponding locked target response. Record that read as a versioned
lineage event.

A grader exposed before the permitted scoring event is disqualified from
the blind primary panel for the affected item or bank. Its output may be
retained only as exploratory evidence.

2. SHAM CLAIM CEILING

SHAM remains a diagnostic subset, not a powered efficacy arm.

It may establish only its frozen tri-status:

    SHAM-DISENGAGED
    SHAM-OPERATIVE
    SHAM-VALID

and may report bounded descriptive contrasts.

It may not establish that ceremonial salience, novelty, placebo-like uptake,
or surface-form effects have been causally eliminated, isolated, or fully
explained. A SHAM result cannot rescue or overturn the primary
LANG-A-versus-SCAFFOLD contrast.

3. DOWNSTREAM CLAIM LINTER

Implement a network-off claim-ceiling linter over every generated branch
receipt, README conclusion, summary, and report abstract.

It must reject unbounded negative or positive templates, including claims
equivalent to:

    Language A works.
    Language A does not work.
    Language A fails.
    The approach is ineffective.
    The pilot proves robustness.
    The validator verified the answer.
    Independent models corroborated the result.
    SHAM ruled out ceremonial effects.
    An inconclusive result is a null result.

It must also require the applicable bounded riders:

- frozen pilot and pilot-scale standing;
- first-pass emission only;
- sampled item bank, subject releases, routes, settings, and run window;
- no inference to hidden reasoning;
- no inference to enforcement efficacy;
- no inference to production custody, global independence, or totality.

Add typed failures such as:

    ForbiddenUnboundedClaim
    MissingClaimCeilingRider
    InconclusiveNarratedAsNull
    LocalizedHarmOvergeneralized
    ShamDiagnosticOverclaimed

Mutation tests must show that each prohibited template is caught.

4. FRESH-CHAIR ADJUDICATION

FREEZE-STAFFING.md must prefer an adjudicator who:

- did not author items, prompts, scaffold, notation rendering, rubric, or
  key;
- did not participate in grader calibration;
- did not see target outputs before the locked first-pass disagreements
  exist;
- receives only the exact adjudication packet;
- operates in a fresh session;
- and, where feasible, uses a different declared model family/provider
  route or a human chair.

Do not claim fresh weights or global adjudicator independence merely from
a fresh session or provider difference.

If no fresh chair is available, adjudication may proceed only with the
shared roots and limitations explicitly recorded. The receipt must not
promote that adjudication to independent corroboration.

5. STRUCTURALLY UNREACHABLE BRANCH TEST

The network-off synthetic precision study must include at least one
canonical favorable synthetic scenario for each substantive branch:

    B-NOTATION
    B-SCAFFOLD
    B-NULL
    B-HARM
    B-INTERACTION

Each scenario must be constructed so that the corresponding branch
predicate is the intended synthetic truth under low-noise or otherwise
favorable declared conditions.

If a substantive branch is selected in none of its canonical favorable
scenarios, and the failure is attributable to the frozen design geometry
rather than an implementation defect, stop before real item-bank freeze
with:

    PILOT-AUTHORITY-RETURN — STRUCTURALLY UNREACHABLE BRANCH

Name the branch, scenario grid, interval behavior, and smallest design
change that would make the branch reachable.

Low but nonzero operating power may be retained as a named feasibility
limitation. Structural impossibility may not be disguised as merely
expecting B-INCONCLUSIVE.

6. EVIDENCE

Record all of the above in the packet's freeze documents, lineage ledger,
mutation tests, synthetic precision report, and verification evidence.

These obligations remain network-off. They do not authorize real-item
authorship by Codex, provider calls, grader exposure, target exposure, or
a pilot verdict.
```

So my answer to Opus is: **yes, this is a very good review**. It recognizes
that the erratum made the experiment harder in precisely the ways that make
eventual results worth reading. Its five remaining tests do not invalidate
the ruling; they tell Codex where the packet must grow teeth.

The pilot is no longer merely hard to birth. It is learning not to
congratulate itself for producing a beautifully formatted stillborn result.
