# strata/ — broken, superseded, and historically important versions

*Sol's shelving law: the damaged and superseded belong here, "rather than being swept away like embarrassing
crockery." Seeded 2026-07-11.*

The canonical residents of this shelf are the **four damaged review-relays** of the Mneme build — the running
proof of conformance fixture #1 (*"a reader must detect that a relay is structurally damaged, preserve the
recoverable claims, and refuse to present the repair as verbatim"*). Each time a document about loss crossed
to a reviewer, it arrived having lost part of itself; the repo copy was clean every time; the relay was the
lossy channel. The stratum:

- v0.4 constitution — arrived as "made of the medd", orphaned headers (`corpus/voices/received/originals/2026-07-11-fable-coldchair-v0.4-review.md` notes the damage)
- the v0.5 synthesis relay — "unverVerdict", amputated clauses
- `continuity.lisp` — "the file would not run" (repo copy exit 0; the paste was damaged)
- `handoff-kernel` review paste — same pattern, fourth occurrence

They are not embarrassments. They are the specimen the whole language keeps generating on itself: *the
constitution for loss-aware handoff is a machine for producing loss-aware conformance fixtures.* When a
`strata/` specimen is written that ingests one of these damaged relays and correctly reports what it can and
cannot recover, fixture #1 earns its first green.

Also belongs here: superseded drafts (`continuity.lisp` is the ancestor of `handoff-kernel.lisp`), and any
specimen retired to the museum but kept for provenance. Keep the dead. Do not let every corpse vote.
