## Why it stays open

The lab already runs this law by hand under a different name — `notes/labeled-holes.md`:
a hole that is FILED is not a hole that is SOLVED. `de-limine.lisp` demonstrates the
narrow case (a polished summary may not erase unresolved fields; the
`unresolved-field-erasure` gate bites). What `de-residuo` would add is the general
instrument: a residue that persists *through* an answer, carried forward as a
first-class object rather than cosmetically closed — the severest executable statement
of *completion of the response is not completion of the world.*

Whoever picks this up: match the atelier's canonization rite (it runs; it states what
it demonstrates; it names what it does not; at least one adversarial gate bites; its
output is an intelligible exhibit; beauty may attend but may not vote). Until then the
remainder stays a remainder — which is the whole point.
