# POST-DE-CORROBORATIONE-PROGRAM-RULING.md

**Selected primary milestone:** freeze and fire a bounded Language-A **emission** pilot, with the frozen branch bank and a complete experiment-side lineage ledger.
The pilot tests first-pass emitted artifacts only; it does not feed validator findings back to the emitting model and therefore does not smuggle enforcement into the emission claim.
Its immediate deliverable is one admissible pilot receipt—positive, scaffold-only, null, harmful, interaction, or inconclusive—plus a go/no-go ruling for a larger confirmatory emission run.

**Optional parallel lane:** build the P2a validator-in-the-loop harness as a nonblocking prototype under the existing firebreak.
That builder may use the public validator, public fixtures, and newly written synthetic examples, but may not see the frozen pilot item bank, scoring keys, randomization schedule, grader packets, or pilot outputs before the primary lane is locked.

**Why this ordering is superior:** the repository has already paid most of the structural debt that the old roadmap placed before later experiments—CD/0 is frozen, LCI/0 is closed and converged, the hardened boundary and typed-condition floors exist, and `de-corroboratione` supplies bounded lineage discipline.
What remains unproved is still the roadmap's central empirical claim: whether assigned Language-A deposition semantics alter the error profile of model emissions relative to matched controls.
A narrow reconciliation is necessary and is completed by this ruling; making reconciliation a separate milestone would turn bookkeeping into another vestibule.

**Explicitly deferred:** a production corroboration runtime, cryptographic custody/signature infrastructure, P2b verdict-bearing enforcement experiments, MCP/JSON interop, broad ontology expansion, certification-risk studies, and any claim of real-world witness independence.
These are not denied forever; they are denied the right to block the first empirical contact.

**Exact claim ceiling:** the pilot may support only a causal statement about the effect of the frozen assigned emission condition on predeclared artifact-level outcomes for the sampled items, model releases, provider routes, decoding settings, and run window.
It may not establish truth, hidden reasoning structure, enforcement efficacy, production provenance, global independence, personnel independence, implementation independence, provider independence, model-family independence, or generalization beyond the declared sample and search field.
Any corroboration wording must be `CORROBORATED-UNDER` named dimensions with guaranteed witnesses, possible upper bounds, shared roots, known contamination, and residual unknowns; naked “independently corroborated” is forbidden.

## 2. Decision and rejected alternatives

### 2.1 Holding

The single primary critical-path lane is:

> **Freeze and fire the Language-A emission pilot.**

“Fire” here means a frozen, bounded pilot whose results may bank a pilot-scale branch. It does not mean silently promoting a cheap teeth-check into a general production claim. The pilot must be capable of returning `B-NOTATION`, `B-SCAFFOLD`, `B-NULL`, `B-HARM`, `B-INTERACTION`, or `B-INCONCLUSIVE` at the size of its own receipt. Any larger confirmatory run requires a successor freeze after the pilot report, but not a new conceptual program.

The decision follows the repository's own strongest design choice: H-emission, H-enforcement, and H-instrument-specificity are distinct falsifiable claims. The project currently has a real notation, a real validator, strong structural foundations, and no empirical emission result. That asymmetry is now comical enough to become diagnostic: the machinery has learned to issue receipts about its receipts, while the central behavioral hypothesis remains outside in the rain.

### 2.3 What would later justify productionizing corroboration

A production corroboration profile becomes justified only when at least one concrete consumer needs machine-checkable ancestry decisions that the experiment ledger cannot safely or repeatedly provide. Sufficient evidence would include all of the following:

1. At least two completed experiments reuse the same lineage dimensions and expose recurring manual-ledger defects, contradictory ancestry folds, or materially expensive re-adjudication.
2. A downstream decision actually depends on a `CORROBORATED-UNDER` receipt rather than merely displaying it as ornament.
3. The required search field, root taxonomy, read-event semantics, exposure chronology, and stopping rule have stabilized across those experiments.
4. A production threat model names who may lie, mutate history, omit reads, forge custody, or conceal provider/model ancestry, and distinguishes what code can detect from what remains declared testimony.
5. Cryptographic identity, key custody, revocation, and replay requirements are specified at the profile boundary without changing CD/0 or LCI/0.
6. A migration plan preserves old bounded receipts and their original graph snapshots rather than reinterpreting them under new ancestry.

Until then, the correct implementation is a small append-only ledger with a loud ceiling. A graph database is not a moral achievement.

## 3. Primary-lane execution packet

### 3.1 Exact objective

The pilot shall estimate whether **assigned first-pass emission condition** changes the rate and shape of predeclared artifact defects on bounded source-packet tasks.

The primary contrast is:

```text
LANG-A minus SCAFFOLD
```

where a negative difference means fewer epistemic defects under Language A. This isolates the notation/semantic representation from the matched structured content. The supporting contrasts are:

```text
SCAFFOLD minus PERSONA
PERSONA minus NL
SHAM minus LANG-A      (diagnostic subset only; not a primary efficacy contrast)
```

No target receives validator feedback, repair instructions, or a second attempt in the emission pilot. Running the validator after collection is measurement. Returning its condition to the target would be enforcement and belongs to P2.

The pilot's causal unit is the exact request envelope: frozen item version, frozen arm material, subject model release, provider route, decoding configuration, and run ordinal. The outcome is the emitted artifact, not the model's hidden process.

### 3.5 Pilot design and arms

#### Item bank

Freeze **24 abstract items**, each backed by a finite, versioned source packet. Use four predeclared families, six items each:

1. **Bounded support:** distinguish supported claims from plausible but unsupported elaboration.
2. **Scope and version:** distinguish a result about one corpus, version, interval, or procedure from a broader claim.
3. **Conflict and residue:** preserve unresolved evidence, disagreement, and unknowns rather than smoothing them into a single confident answer.
4. **Notation-neutral transfer:** apply the discipline in a task whose answer format does not resemble Language A, testing whether the method travels beyond memorized liturgy.

At least eight items must contain deliberate insufficiency where the lawful artifact withholds or bounds a conclusion. At least eight must contain a predeclared trap from the following closed classes: unsupported assertion, scope extension, version laundering, receipt-as-truth, self-certified verification, erased unresolved residue, unnamed effectful procedure, or confidence without an admissible basis. Each trap has a catchability witness written before subject exposure. Trap success supports only performance on this trap family and carries the trap-circularity rider.

No item may be copied, paraphrased, or mechanically permuted from:

- the fourteen public Language-A fixtures;
- any P2a prototype example;
- any CD/0 or LCI/0 conformance vector;
- any example emitted during harness development;
- any item seen by a grader model during rubric calibration.

The item author records source ancestry and prior exposure. The freezer performs a lexical-collision and semantic-overlap audit before freezing.

#### Core arms

All 24 items run in four paired arms for each subject slot:

- **NL:** task and source packet only, with ordinary answer instruction.
- **PERSONA:** NL plus a careful-auditor role instruction, without required sections or notation.
- **SCAFFOLD:** a prose-labeled answer template carrying the same substantive obligations as Language A—answer, claims, support, procedure, confidence, boundaries, and unresolved residue—without Lisp syntax, Language-A names, validator terms, or typed-condition names.
- **LANG-A:** the frozen Language-A deposition instruction and record form, using the existing doctrine and validator vocabulary.

The SCAFFOLD and LANG-A materials must be content-matched at the obligation level. Their preamble and teaching burden must be reported in UTF-8 bytes, words, and provider-reported input tokens. Content parity is engineered; representational parity is impossible because representation is the manipulated variable.

#### Sham diagnostic

Eight predeclared items—two from each family—also run under **SHAM**. SHAM matches LANG-A's novelty, density, section count, and approximate token burden while using inert operators and labels that do not encode the actual support/boundary semantics.

The sham is assigned one of three frozen statuses:

- `SHAM-DISENGAGED`: required field/structure uptake is below 70% or subjects explicitly discard it as meaningless.
- `SHAM-OPERATIVE`: blind inspection finds that the sham leaks or can be reverse-engineered into a task-relevant strategy.
- `SHAM-VALID`: uptake is at least 70%, instruction-token burden is within 10% of LANG-A for each provider tokenizer, and the semantic-leak audit finds no task-relevant guidance.

Only `SHAM-VALID` licenses a clean ceremonial-salience comparison. The sham result cannot rescue a failed primary LANG-A–SCAFFOLD contrast.

#### Subject slots and run size

Freeze exactly **three subject slots**, each an exact model release and endpoint configuration. They must cover at least three declared model families and at least two provider routes, with at least one non-Claude family. This is coverage, not a pre-judged independence claim.

Scheduled target calls:

```text
24 items × 4 core arms × 3 subject slots = 288
 8 items × 1 sham arm  × 3 subject slots =  24
                                               ---
                                              312 scheduled calls
```

Allow at most 32 transport retries, for a hard ceiling of **344 attempted provider calls**. A retry is a new logged observation linked to its failed parent; no failed attempt is overwritten. Content, policy, refusal, or truncation outcomes are not transport failures and are never silently retried.

Every call uses a fresh conversation/session, no cross-call memory, no tools, no retrieval, no hidden system material beyond the frozen provider wrapper, and the lowest deterministic setting the endpoint actually supports. Exact temperature, top-p, seed behavior, maximum output tokens, safety mode, tool state, and provider wrapper are frozen per subject slot. “Temperature 0” is not described as deterministic unless replay demonstrates it.

Call order is generated from one frozen seed and blocked by subject, item family, and arm. The complete schedule is hashed before exposure. Provider caching is disabled where an explicit control exists; otherwise cache status is recorded as unknown or provider-reported, never assumed absent.

### 3.7 Outcomes and estimands

The primary per-call outcome is **epistemic defect burden**:

```text
(unsupported assertions
 + scope errors
 + version errors
 + erased unresolved residues)
÷ predeclared scorable opportunities
```

Every component has item-key rules frozen before exposure. Counts are kept separately; the composite does not erase which defect moved.

Secondary outcomes are:

- unsupported-assertion rate;
- scope/version overreach rate;
- unresolved-residue survival rate;
- trap-catch rate, with catchability and circularity riders;
- answer utility;
- native-artifact inspectability;
- Language-A validator pass/typed-condition profile, applicable only to LANG-A artifacts;
- decorative-compliance rate: structurally compliant artifacts that retain at least one keyed epistemic defect;
- refusal, deletion, truncation, and abstention rates;
- input/output token burden, latency, and cost;
- predeclared family and subject interactions.

Define the paired contrasts:

```text
D_N = burden_LANG-A  − burden_SCAFFOLD
D_S = burden_SCAFFOLD − burden_PERSONA
D_P = burden_PERSONA − burden_NL
```

Negative is better in every contrast. The freezer shall lock these pilot-scale practical margins:

```text
meaningful improvement margin δ = 0.10 absolute burden
practical equivalence band ε = ±0.05 absolute burden
harm margin h = 0.10 absolute burden
```

These are policy thresholds, not estimates learned from pilot output. Analysis uses paired item×subject differences, a fixed-seed nonparametric bootstrap confidence interval, and a paired sign/randomization test implemented in the archived analysis code. Exact resampling seed and iteration count are frozen. Domain and subject interactions are reported from the predeclared strata; no post-hoc subgroup becomes a new branch.

The branch rules are:

- **B-NOTATION:** the 95% interval for `D_N` lies wholly at or below `−δ`; LANG-A has no predeclared utility, refusal, or cost harm; core manipulation checks pass.
- **B-SCAFFOLD:** the 95% interval for `D_S` lies wholly at or below `−δ`, while the 95% interval for `D_N` lies wholly inside `[-ε,+ε]`; no harm gate fires.
- **B-NULL:** the 95% intervals for `D_N`, `D_S`, and `D_P` each lie wholly inside `[-ε,+ε]`, with adequate analyzable cells and passing manipulation checks.
- **B-HARM:** a core arm worsens burden by at least `h` with the 95% interval wholly beyond the harm margin, or crosses a predeclared refusal/utility/cost harm threshold. The harmed contrast and domain are named; harm is not generalized to every surface.
- **B-INTERACTION:** the predeclared family interaction exceeds 0.15 absolute burden and at least two family-specific contrasts have opposite signs with intervals excluding zero. This is not a euphemism for miscellaneous noise.
- **B-INCONCLUSIVE:** precision is inadequate, a core manipulation check fails, analyzable-cell minima fail, results do not satisfy another branch, or lineage/scoring limitations prevent the stronger branch. Inconclusive is a result, not permission to narrate the most flattering point estimate.

Because this is a pilot, every branch is explicitly suffixed “at pilot scale.” A production claim requires a successor preregistration informed by feasibility and cost, not by re-optimizing the hypothesis around the observed sign.

### 3.8 Negative controls and manipulation checks

#### Negative controls

1. **Easy bounded-control items:** all arms should recover the same plainly stated fact. A large arm difference indicates instruction burden or parser damage rather than epistemic benefit.
2. **Insufficient-evidence controls:** the keyed response must preserve an unknown or bounded absence. Fluent completion is counted as an error.
3. **Format-irrelevant controls:** direct calculations or lookups where notation should confer no substantive advantage. A large gain suggests uncontrolled teaching or scoring bias.
4. **Validator mutants:** synthetic Language-A records each carrying one known fixture-class defect; the experiment-local driver must return the expected first typed condition.
5. **Scorer mutants:** altered outputs with one inserted unsupported assertion, scope widening, version swap, residue deletion, or wholesale answer deletion. The scorer must wound the named dimension and must not reward repair-by-incineration.
6. **Manifest mutants:** an unmanifested file, stale hash, renamed path, early-termination completion claim, and duplicate call ID must each produce a loud typed failure.

#### Manipulation checks

- LANG-A and SCAFFOLD structural uptake must each be at least 70% overall and at least 50% in every subject slot; otherwise the affected contrast is inconclusive.
- Obligation-level parity between SCAFFOLD and LANG-A is reviewed before freeze by a person who did not author either rendering.
- UTF-8 byte count and word count must be within 10% between SCAFFOLD and LANG-A; provider-reported input-token deltas are analyzed rather than retrospectively “matched.”
- The sham receives one and only one tri-status under the frozen rules.
- A lexical-collision report names shared keywords, prohibited Language-A identifiers in SCAFFOLD, and any sham token that accidentally conveys support semantics.
- At least one notation-neutral transfer item per family must be answered without a required Language-A surface form; otherwise the transfer claim is not exercised.
- Trap catchability is demonstrated by a lawful reference artifact and one deliberately failing artifact before exposure.
- Grader blinding is checked by asking graders to guess arm on the content-normalized view. Native-format inspectability scoring remains knowingly unblinded because the format is the object being scored.

### 3.10 Scoring and grader protocol

Scoring occurs in two views:

- **Content-normalized view:** preserves every substantive word and source citation but removes filenames, arm labels, and purely decorative delimiters. It is used for unsupported claims, scope/version errors, residue, trap outcomes, and utility.
- **Native-artifact view:** preserves the emitted form and is used for inspectability, structural uptake, validator behavior, and decorative compliance. Arm visibility is an admitted limitation here.

Each call receives deterministic keyed scoring plus two blind rubric passes for the primary defect categories. Disagreements are adjudicated under the frozen rubric by an adjudicator who did not author the item. The pilot may proceed to branch analysis only if the duplicate-scored calibration set achieves both:

```text
raw categorical agreement ≥ 0.80
Cohen's kappa ≥ 0.60 for each primary categorical defect family
```

If prevalence makes kappa undefined, raw agreement and the full confusion table are reported; the freezer must predeclare the replacement gate before target scoring begins. Continuous count disagreement greater than one defect is always adjudicated. No grader may train on the target outputs before producing its locked first pass.

Model graders are witnesses with declared provider/family routes, not oracles. Their agreement does not mint truth. Keyed finite source packets and deterministic rules remain the primary scoring authority; rubric judgments retain their own uncertainty and lineage profile.

### 3.13 Expected failure modes

The packet must expect, not cosmetically discover, these failures:

- baseline error is too rare, leaving no headroom;
- Language-A artifacts are syntactically compliant but substantively decorative;
- SCAFFOLD carries nearly all benefit, leaving notation without a measurable increment;
- SHAM is ignored, detected, or accidentally operative;
- instruction burden causes refusal, truncation, or utility loss;
- source packets or score keys encode the item author's preferred ontology too directly;
- graders infer arm from language and import preference into substantive scoring;
- the validator catches structural incoherence but cannot catch a cooperative lie;
- model/provider versions drift between freeze and fire;
- hidden provider caching or routing changes make repeated calls non-comparable;
- a normalizer drops text, residue, or delimiters and creates arm-asymmetric scoring;
- a retry policy converts content failures into selectively cleaner data;
- one model family dominates the aggregate and hides a predeclared interaction;
- token/cost fields are absent or inconsistent;
- lineage declarations show shared roots that narrow the corroboration dimensions;
- precision is insufficient for positive, equivalence, or harm margins.

Every one has a branch, stop condition, or ceiling. None licenses deletion of the awkward run.

### 3.14 Acceptance gate

The pilot is admissible for branch banking only when all of the following hold:

1. The pre-exposure freeze manifest, preregistration, branch rules, staffing identities, exact model/endpoint configuration, randomization schedule, key hash, and lineage search field are locked.
2. `verify-pilot.sh` passes twice from fresh temporary directories and its manifest-mutant tests fail loudly as expected.
3. No protected file differs from the captured base.
4. At least 90% of the 312 scheduled cells are analyzable overall, at least 80% in every core arm×subject×family stratum, and every missing or failed cell remains in the census.
5. There are zero silent retries, duplicate call IDs, overwritten raw responses, unexplained model-ID substitutions, or unreconciled hash mismatches.
6. The subject models and P2a builder did not receive the score key; the primary runner did not have filesystem access to it; all authorized reads are recorded.
7. The scorer and validator-driver mutation controls pass; no surviving mutant affects an unnamed dimension.
8. Grader reliability clears the frozen gate or the result is banked `B-INCONCLUSIVE` with the complete disagreement matrix.
9. The relevant manipulation checks have determinate status. A failed core manipulation check forces `B-INCONCLUSIVE`; it is not waved away because the point estimate is attractive.
10. The complete analysis reruns from raw immutable responses and the frozen key to the same branch receipt and digest.
11. Total attempted calls and spend remain within their ceilings.
12. The lineage search field reaches its stated stopping rule; residual unknowns are carried into the receipt.

Passing this gate means the pilot is interpretable at its stated ceiling. It does not mean Language A “works.” A banked null, harm, interaction, or inconclusive branch passes the integrity gate just as a notation-positive branch does.

### 5.2 Worked hypothetical

Suppose the pilot uses three graders:

- `grader-alpha`, provider Alpha, declared model family Cedar;
- `grader-beta`, provider Beta, declared model family Juniper;
- `grader-gamma`, provider Gamma, declared model family Larch, with an undisclosed subcontracted inference backend.

Alpha and Beta are from different providers and declare different model families, but both read the exact frozen rubric `R1`. Therefore:

- along **provider-routing**, Alpha and Beta can form the guaranteed witness subset if the closed access and endpoint records disclose no shared provider route;
- along **model-family routing**, they are merely declared different families; hidden weight/training ancestry remains unknown unless independently evidenced;
- along **rubric framing** and **error taxonomy**, they share a relevant root, so their agreement is not independent on those dimensions;
- along **truth**, none of them is an authority merely by agreeing.

## 7. Stop rules

### 7.1 Ordinary repair — `PILOT-LOCAL-REPAIR`

Ordinary repair is allowed without refreezing only when the defect cannot alter any target-visible bytes, item assignment, score-key semantics, branch rule, analysis estimand, lineage relation, or raw evidence. Examples:

- a report renderer has a typo;
- a dashboard misorders already locked rows while the underlying JSONL is correct;
- a nonsemantic path pointer in README is wrong;
- an evidence-copy script omits a derived file but all originals and hashes remain intact.

The repair is a versioned successor with a regression test. No subject call is rerun merely to make the report prettier.

### 7.2 Experiment refreeze — `PILOT-REFREEZE-REQUIRED`

Refreeze is mandatory before further target calls if any of these change:

- prompt or source-packet bytes;
- item wording, trap, catchability witness, scoring opportunity, or exclusion status;
- arm semantics, sham construction, or manipulation threshold;
- score key, rubric, adjudication rule, branch margin, analysis code, or resampling seed;
- subject model ID, endpoint, provider wrapper, decoding settings, retry policy, or schedule;
- lineage dimensions, search field, stopping rule, or role access;
- normalizer behavior that can change scored content.

All pre-refreeze exposures remain archived under the old version and are excluded or retained only by the frozen inclusion rule. They are never relabeled as if generated under the successor packet.

### 7.3 Branch quarantine — `PILOT-BRANCH-QUARANTINED`

Quarantine the affected branch or run immediately when:

- a protected scope changes;
- an item, key, schedule, or rubric digest mismatches its freeze manifest;
- the P2a builder or a subject receives prohibited P1 material;
- the scoring key is exposed before output lock to an unauthorized actor;
- exploratory and confirmatory outputs are pooled;
- a raw response is overwritten or cannot be matched to one request;
- replay diverges from the locked schedule, scorer, or analysis receipt;
- model/provider substitution occurs without a predeclared rule;
- a completion claim appears after early termination or missing cells are silently dropped.

Quarantine preserves all artifacts and stops their use in the main estimate. It is not deletion and not automatically cancellation; a bounded unaffected branch may continue only if the preregistered inclusion rule and minimum census permit it.

### 7.4 Authorial ruling — `PILOT-AUTHORITY-RETURN`

Stop for an authorial ruling when the builder encounters:

- a concrete contradiction between controlling documents;
- an ambiguity that changes whether a defect is unsupported assertion, scope error, version error, or lawful residue;
- a need to alter the Language-A validator or first-condition precedence;
- a branch case not decided by `BRANCH-BANK.md`;
- a claim-ceiling question whose answer would enlarge the experiment's semantics;
- a proposed use of CD/0, LCI/0, warrant, or corroboration semantics outside their accepted boundaries.

The return names the exact artifact, lines or data shape, smallest witness, and blocked decision. “More review would be nice” is not an authority return.

### 7.5 Cancellation — `PILOT-CANCELLED`

Cancel the current frozen pilot, archive it, and do not rescue it when:

- the item or score key is broadly compromised and a bounded unaffected bank cannot be identified under the frozen rule;
- required raw outputs cannot legally or technically be retained and replayed;
- fewer than the minimum analyzable cells can be obtained within 344 attempts or USD 8.00;
- provider behavior prevents exact model/route identification to the degree required by the claim ceiling;
- a deterministic scorer or validator adapter is shown to invert or erase a primary outcome after exposures, with no preregistered unaffected estimate;
- the randomization schedule or raw-response census cannot be reconstructed;
- the declared lineage search field cannot reach its stopping rule because required actors or artifact deliveries are unavailable.

Cancellation is not a null result. It is a failed experiment receipt with reasons and retained evidence.

### 7.6 Publish null or harm without rescue — `PILOT-OUTCOME-BANKED`

When the frozen run is admissible and `B-NULL` or `B-HARM` fires, publish that branch at pilot scale. Do not:

- drop the model family or domain carrying the negative result unless the frozen exclusion rule already requires it;
- redefine “error,” “valid,” or “utility” after seeing the outcome;
- promote a secondary metric because the primary disappointed;
- call scaffold benefit “Language-A benefit”;
- call inadequate precision a null;
- launch a redesigned run and present it as continuation of the same preregistration;
- suppress the result because P2a looks more promising.

A successor study may test a new hypothesis, but it cites the null or harmful predecessor as ancestry rather than laundering it away.
