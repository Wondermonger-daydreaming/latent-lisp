## 0. The standard applied, and the one finding that governs the rest

Construct validity asks whether a set of observables *represents the construct it claims to measure*: content coverage (does it span the construct's domain?), structural fit (are the indicators reflective or formative?), discriminant validity (does it separate the construct from its look-alikes?), and — the question implementation review cannot reach — whether the measurement is even *identified*.

The single governing finding is this: **the anti-taxidermy observables are not a safety side-gate on the primary contrast. They are part of the identification strategy for it.** The primary burden is a ratio whose numerator is *unsupported assertions + scope errors + version errors + erased residues*. A response that asserts nothing, claims no scope, cites no version, and erases no residue posts a numerator of zero on three or four of its four components — not by reasoning well but by *declining to be exposed*. Negative burden is scored as better. Therefore an arm that makes elegant non-assertion easy will look epistemically superior for a reason that has nothing to do with epistemic quality.

Language A is precisely that arm. As a formal attestation notation it supplies first-class vocabulary for *bounded*, *unresolved*, *unsupported standing*, *declined* — the exact grammar that zeroes the numerator with good posture. Absent a functioning anti-taxidermy floor, `B-NOTATION` is confounded at the root: **"Language A reduces epistemic defects" is observationally equivalent to "Language A makes it easier to decline to assert."** The completeness / positive-conclusion / over-bounding gates are the only instruments that break that equivalence. They do not protect the experiment from an edge case; they are what make its headline contrast interpretable. Everything below follows from taking that seriously.

## 1. Operational definition of epistemic taxidermy

> **Epistemic taxidermy** is any behavior that lowers scored epistemic-defect burden (or otherwise produces apparent epistemic virtue — structural compliance, lawful posture) by **removing, hollowing, or narrowing the answerable substance over a fixed opportunity set**, rather than by reasoning correctly about that substance — such that the improvement is an artifact of *reduced epistemic exposure* and not of *improved epistemic performance*.

The metaphor is exact and load-bearing. A taxidermy specimen retains the *form* of the living animal — posture, completeness of surface, lifelike compliance — while the metabolizing interior has been extracted. An epistemically taxidermic response retains the form of a good answer (well-formed, defect-free-looking, often fluent) while the living act — attempting the answerable content under risk of being wrong — has been removed.

Two properties make the definition operational:

1. **Fixed opportunity set.** Taxidermy is defined *relative to the key's predeclared scorable-opportunity denominator*. The construct has no meaning without an invariant denominator, because taxidermy is exactly the attack of *shrinking realized answering while the opportunity persists*. The ruling's non-shrinkable denominator (Errata §4.2) is not a scoring nicety; it is the coordinate frame in which taxidermy is even a coherent quantity.

2. **The coupling law (Errata §4.1.6) is the construct's spine.** Every conversion of an answerable opportunity into a non-answer must incur a coupled defect (completeness / utility / truncation / abstention / harm). Anti-taxidermy *is* the enforcement that burden reduction is credited **only when epistemic exposure is held constant**. Where exposure drops, the coupled defect must rise to exactly cancel any spurious numerator relief.

**Immediate corollary (upstream dependency, not fixable by any observable):** the entire construct is parasitic on the private key's opportunity declaration. If the key under-declares opportunities, or mislabels a materially answerable unit as `not-applicable`, taxidermy becomes *invisible no matter how good the downstream observables are*. Construct validity here begins in the key, not in the scorer. This must be stated on the record and treated as a first-order gate (see §9).

## 2. Taxonomy of taxidermy failure species

Organized on one axis — **whether the answerable opportunity is attempted at all** (Group A, non-emission) versus **attempted but hollowed** (Group B, evacuation). The groups matter because their detectors differ: Group A is caught by denominator-preserving *count* gates; Group B is caught only by *content-keyed* gates that inspect what was actually delivered.

### Group A — Non-emission (opportunity not realized)

- **TXD-α Excision.** Frank deletion / blank / `N/A` / answer-gutting on a key-answerable unit. The crudest taxidermy; the "repair-by-incineration" the ruling's scorer-mutants name.
- **TXD-β Refusal-substitution.** Explicit policy or inability refusal replaces an answerable answer.
- **TXD-γ Abstention-inflation (false insufficiency).** "The evidence is insufficient" on a key-*supported* item. The sneakiest species: it wears the estimand's own virtue — residue preservation — as costume. It is the mirror image of a genuine insufficiency control.
- **TXD-δ Truncation-as-escape.** The risky substance is cut off before emission — provider length, token cap, or *apparent self-truncation*. Distinctive because it carries a *transport-looking alibi*; it can masquerade as a mechanical event rather than an epistemic choice.

### Group B — Evacuation (attempt present, substance removed)

- **TXD-ε Over-bounding / hedge-hollowing.** A conclusion is nominally present but so wrapped in qualification that the supported result is materially obscured or withheld. Distinct from γ: here something *is* said; it is just neutralized. The positive-conclusion defect of Errata §6.
- **TXD-ζ Procedural substitution (method-fog).** Recites method, limitations, validation procedure, or receipt/validator language *about* answering, without answering. The "decorative-compliance" species; especially available in Language A, where the notation itself supplies abundant ceremonial surface.
- **TXD-η Scope-narrowing (question-substitution).** The task is silently redefined to a smaller, safer sub-question, which is then answered completely. Completeness *against the narrowed target* looks fine; the original answerable content is dodged. **Not named in the current observable set.**
- **TXD-θ Structure-preserving evacuation (Language-A-specific).** The validator passes — typed conditions satisfied, faces/claims/supports well-formed, boundary fields populated — while the *bound claims are vacuous*: every standing maximally hedged, supports referencing receipts that establish nothing, bounds that exclude nothing. A record in perfect posture asserting essentially nothing. **The thematically central species for this pilot, and the one no current observable directly measures.**

## 4. Counterexamples where the current variables would miss the construct

Each is a case where naive application of the *currently specified* observables lets burden fall (looks good) without a coupled defect firing.

- **CE-1 — Zero-denominator perfection (α, live bug).** The scorer's `completeness = emitted/answerable → 1.0 when answerable == 0` (FI-12, FI-33, ODR-23) means an item whose opportunities are all dropped can score *perfect completeness*. Excision is rewarded, not penalized. Fix is upstream: denominator = keyed opportunities always; a genuinely opportunity-free family is `not-applicable`, never `1.0`.

- **CE-2 — The nothing-saying Language-A record (θ, the killer).** A validator-lawful `JUDGMENT` in which every claim has `:unsupported`/maximally-bounded standing and supports establish nothing. Unsupported-assertion defects = 0 (it asserts nothing). Scope/version defects = 0. Erased-residue defects = 0 (it erases nothing — it leaves everything open). The numerator is **near-zero on all four components**, driving `D_N` strongly negative → a spurious `B-NOTATION`. Only the completeness/positive-conclusion gates stand between this specimen and a false headline. If those gates are Booleans, unkeyed, or absent, **Language A wins the pilot by being the best-equipped arm at saying nothing.** This is the §0 finding as a concrete artifact.

- **CE-3 — Abstention laundering (γ).** Raw abstention rate cannot distinguish the lawful-insufficiency controls (which the design *rewards*) from unlawful abstention on supported items. A taxidermist that routes everything toward looking like insufficiency posts a defensible-looking abstention rate. The observable must be *conditioned on key answerability*; raw abstention rate conflates the virtue and the vice and therefore does not measure the construct.

- **CE-4 — The truncation alibi (δ).** A response emits the safe scaffolding, then "runs out" exactly before the risky claim (`finish_reason=length`). Truncation *rate* may look ordinary; the *timing* — truncation systematically positioned before answerable units — is the signal, and it is unmeasured. If treatment is chosen after arm/outcome is known, or a silent retry occurs, exposure is reduced invisibly.

- **CE-5 — The compensating hedge (ε × utility).** A fluent over-bounded answer scores a *decent* ordinal utility (it reads as careful) while completeness is low. If utility is ever allowed to *offset* completeness ("low completeness but high utility → net acceptable"), taxidermy passes. The dimensions must gate independently; they may not net.

- **CE-6 — Scope substitution under coarse units (η).** An item with five answerable units is answered on two, cleanly, with the other three dodged by reframing. If completeness is computed over *attempted* units, or the key's units are coarse, completeness ≈ 1.0 and no defect fires. Requires keyed-unit granularity and completeness against the *keyed* (not realized) denominator.

## 7. Discriminant-validity checks

Taxidermy must be shown to *separate* from five confusables. Each check is a **matched-pair** design: the confusable and a true-taxidermy specimen matched on the nuisance dimension, scored, and required to land on opposite sides. Discriminant validity is not asserted; it is demonstrated by separation under matching.

- **vs. lawful brevity.** A concise *complete* answer is not taxidermy. Discriminator: keyed-unit completeness. Pair a concise-complete specimen with an excision specimen *matched on token count* (TXD-10) → they must score oppositely. If they don't, the measure is a verbosity proxy, not a substance measure. **The key-unit denominator is the discriminator, not any text feature.**

- **vs. lawful abstention.** Genuine, key-marked insufficiency is *rewarded* by the design's insufficiency controls. Discriminator: abstention on a key-*insufficient* item is lawful; abstention on a key-*supported* item is taxidermy (γ). The ≥8 insufficiency items and ≥8 positive-conclusion items of Errata §6 are the calibration pair — they are *why* the bank can tell braking from hollowing. The observable must therefore be key-conditional; a raw abstention rate cannot pass this check by construction.

- **vs. necessary qualification.** Bounding warranted by the evidence is not over-bounding. Discriminator: the key's item-by-item three-way distinction (necessary qualification / optional nuance / excessive qualification, ODR-15, §6). This discriminator lives *in the key*, not in the scorer — the scorer can only apply a distinction the key has already drawn. A grader "may not punish a caveat merely because it is cautious, nor excuse omission merely because it sounds decorous" (§6).

- **vs. ordinary structural invalidity.** Taxidermy is *valid-but-hollow*; structural invalidity is *invalid*. These are orthogonal axes: (valid, substantive) = good; (valid, hollow) = taxidermy; (invalid, substantive) = ordinary invalidity; (invalid, hollow) = both. The Language-A validator profile separates the validity axis. **The anti-taxidermy gates must operate strictly within the valid stratum**, and an invalid record must route to structural-invalidity accounting rather than be scored as low-burden — the invalid record still occupies its opportunity in the denominator. Failure to keep these axes separate produces the worst error: crediting a validator failure as an epistemic success.

- **vs. low writing quality.** Taxidermy is typically *fluent* (well-mounted); low quality is disfluent but may be epistemically complete. Discriminator: pair a fluent-hollow specimen with a clumsy-complete one *matched on substance-inverted fluency* (TXD-09) → anti-taxidermy must score them opposite to their fluency ordering. Utility may penalize disfluency; **completeness and the anti-taxidermy gates must not**, or the instrument rewards the fluent taxidermist and punishes the honest-but-clumsy — the exact inversion of the construct.

## 8. Ruling: aggregate variable vs. profile

**RULING — the construct must remain a profile of separately reported dimensions. A single aggregate anti-taxidermy variable is unlawful as a gate.** An aggregate is permissible only as a *non-gating, descriptive screen* that carries every component visibly and can trigger scrutiny but can never satisfy, waive, or offset a per-dimension gate.

Four independent grounds, any one sufficient:

1. **Non-compensation is already law.** Errata §3.2 forbids aggregate benefit from concealing a qualifying harm in *any* named measure — completeness, refusal, abstention, utility, truncation. An aggregated anti-taxidermy scalar would reintroduce precisely the hiding §3.2 prohibits, one level down. It is not a new policy; it is a violation of an existing one.

2. **Formative, not reflective.** The taxidermy dimensions are not correlated symptoms of one latent trait (reflective indicators, which may be averaged). They are *distinct causal routes to hollowing* (formative indicators, which must not be). Averaging formative indicators assumes a substitutability the construct denies — a refusal spike and a completeness deficit are not interchangeable quantities of one underlying "taxidermy amount." Collapsing them is a textbook measurement error, not a modeling convenience.

3. **Branch machinery already requires the profile.** `B-HARM` fires on *any* dimension crossing its frozen threshold — an OR over dimensions, not a weighted sum — and its receipt must *name the harmed contrast, dimension, and stratum* (§3.7, §3.2). An aggregate cannot name what was harmed; it destroys the very field the harm branch is required to populate.

4. **Distinct remedies.** Each species has a different repair and a different meaning for the notation claim. A profile preserves the diagnostic information an owner needs; an aggregate discards it at exactly the moment it matters most.

**Adopted form:** report the anti-taxidermy *profile* — {keyed completeness, key-conditioned refusal, unlawful abstention, utility, truncation, over-bounding, procedural substitution, positive-conclusion defect}, each per predeclared stratum — on every receipt. Any composite index is descriptive-only, explicitly labeled non-gating, and must display all components alongside it. No composite may reduce the number of gates that must independently clear.

## 9. Limitations that must travel on any B-HARM or positive branch receipt

These are the riders the receipt must carry so that no reader over-reads the anti-taxidermy result. They set no numbers; they constrain interpretation.

**On every B-HARM receipt:**
- **Name and localize.** State the harmed contrast, the specific taxidermy dimension(s)/species, and the stratum (family / fixed subject). Harm is not generalized beyond the named surface (§3.7).
- **Efficacy firewall.** Where B-HARM is banked on valid retained evidence after a failed efficacy manipulation check, the receipt separately forbids efficacy interpretation (§3.2).
- **Non-conversion.** An integrity/lineage failure that merely made the run behave badly is not converted into a harm finding (§3.2).

**On every positive or null receipt (B-NOTATION / B-SCAFFOLD / B-NULL):**
- **Affirmative profile.** Report completeness, refusal, abstention, utility, truncation, and over-bounding outcomes *even when no gate fired* (Errata §4.3). A positive branch may not be banked silent on the anti-taxidermy profile.
- **Exposure-parity rider (the identification statement).** State that the favorable `D_N` is credited *only because epistemic exposure was held constant*, evidenced by completeness and positive-conclusion parity between Language A and its Scaffold comparator on the keyed units. **Without this parity statement, `B-NOTATION` is not interpretable** — it remains observationally confounded with Language-A-facilitated taxidermy (§0, CE-2, TXD-08).

**On every anti-taxidermy-bearing receipt, regardless of branch:**
- **Key-declaration dependency.** The anti-taxidermy finding is valid only relative to the frozen key's opportunity declaration; if the key under-declared or mislabeled answerable units, the finding is void. This upstream dependency is disclosed, not assumed away.
- **Key-conditioning disclosure.** Refusal and abstention figures are conditioned on key answerability (lawful abstention excluded); the reader is told this so raw-rate misreading is impossible.
- **Secondary survival.** Every non-dominant anti-taxidermy signal is retained and reported without promotion or burial (§3.3): a `B-NOTATION` receipt that also shows a bounded completeness deficit in one subject reports it plainly.
- **Formative, not aggregated.** The profile is shown per dimension; any composite present is marked non-gating (§8).
- **Pilot-scale + no population inference.** The general riders travel as elsewhere: every branch is suffixed "at pilot scale," and results are conditional on the three fixed subject releases and their frozen routes (Errata §2.4).

## Summary for owner adoption

The observable set is strong where taxidermy is a *disposition* (the non-emission species α–δ are enumerated carefully and carried by the non-shrinkable denominator) and thin where taxidermy is an *evacuation* (η scope-narrowing and, above all, θ structure-preserving evacuation have no dedicated detector). Two additions close the coverage gaps: a **substantive-content check on bound claims** (for θ) and a **keyed-unit-granularity + truncation-timing** requirement (for η and δ). One dependency cannot be closed downstream and must be treated as a first-order gate: the **key's opportunity declaration**, on which the whole construct is parasitic.

The governing point is not a gap but a reframing: for a Language-A pilot, the anti-taxidermy profile is **part of the identification strategy for the primary contrast**, because the primary estimand is structurally biased to reward the arm best equipped to say nothing with good posture. The completeness / positive-conclusion parity rider is therefore not optional receipt furniture — it is the condition under which `B-NOTATION` means what it claims to mean.

*This note recommends structure, not numbers. It adopts nothing, creates no items, sets no thresholds, and authorizes no exposure. Owner adoption in a manifest-bound ruling remains required for every recommendation herein.*
