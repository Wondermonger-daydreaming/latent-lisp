# Scoring specification

This construction scorer accepts explicit synthetic facts only. The future real key remains operator-held and absent.

Primary defect categories are unsupported assertion, scope error, version error, and erased unresolved residue. Per-call burden is their sum divided by the fixed predeclared opportunity count. Omission, refusal, truncation, or non-instantiation never lowers that denominator.

The content-normalized view removes arm labels and decorative delimiters but preserves substantive words and citations. It owns primary categories, traps, positive-conclusion defects, and utility. The native view owns structural uptake, validator behavior, inspectability, and decorative compliance. Native scoring is knowingly unblinded.

Each key must distinguish required supported conclusions and answerable units, necessary qualification, genuinely unsupported/unresolved content, and optional exposition. It separately scores unnecessary abstention, excessive qualification, omitted supported conclusion, and procedural-caveat substitution. Deletion, refusal, blanket uncertainty, truncation, omission, or gutting must set a coupled anti-taxidermy defect even when primary assertion counts fall.

Two locked blind first passes and item-level source packet reads follow the firebreak in PREREG v0.2 and FREEZE-STAFFING. Agreement floor is 0.80 and kappa floor 0.60 per primary categorical family; the undefined-kappa substitute remains owner-unresolved. Count disagreements above one are adjudicated.

The deterministic Language-A validator applies only to LANG-A native artifacts and checks coherence only. It does not verify truth, source support, calibration, or hidden cognition.
