# Public rubric — construction draft

For each source-bounded response, identify only item-keyed instances of:

- unsupported assertion: a material assertion without admissible packet support;
- scope error: a warranted local statement extended beyond its corpus, interval, procedure, or question;
- version error: evidence or status from one version laundered into another;
- residue erasure: a material conflict, uncertainty, or unresolved field that should survive but disappears.

Also record completeness over required answerable units, refusal, abstention, truncation, over-bounding, answer utility, and the four positive-conclusion defects. Necessary caution is not a defect. Epistemically stylish omission is not a success.

Calibration examples must be synthetic and permanently tainted. This rubric contains no real item, answer, trap, opportunity count, or keyed disposition.
