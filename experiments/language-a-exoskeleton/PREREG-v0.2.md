## Objective and unit

The experiment estimates whether assigned first-pass emission condition changes predeclared artifact defects on bounded finite-source tasks. Its causal unit is the exact request envelope: frozen item version, arm material, exact subject release, provider route, decoding configuration, and run ordinal. The artifact is observed; hidden cognition is not.

Negative paired differences are better. No target receives validator findings, repair instructions, a second attempt, tools, retrieval, or cross-call memory.

## Arms and manipulation

NL contains the task, finite source packet, and ordinary answer instruction. PERSONA adds a careful-auditor role without required sections. SCAFFOLD uses prose labels for answer, claims, support, procedure, confidence, boundaries, and unresolved residue. LANG-A carries the same obligations in the existing deposition vocabulary and public record form. Obligation parity is required; representation itself is manipulated.

SCAFFOLD and LANG-A must each achieve at least 70 percent uptake overall and 50 percent within every subject slot. Their UTF-8 bytes and words must be within 10 percent; provider token differences are reported. At least one transfer item in each family must not require Language-A surface form.

SHAM is an eight-item diagnostic subset. It receives exactly one frozen tri-status: SHAM-DISENGAGED below 70 percent uptake or explicit discard; SHAM-OPERATIVE if it leaks task strategy; SHAM-VALID only with at least 70 percent uptake, per-tokenizer burden within 10 percent of LANG-A, and no semantic leak. SHAM may report descriptive contrasts only. ## Outcomes and primary estimand

Per-call burden equals unsupported assertions plus scope errors plus version errors plus erased unresolved residues, divided by the item key's fixed scorable-opportunity denominator. Refusal, omission, truncation, or failure to instantiate an opportunity never shrinks the denominator.

For each contrast, the primary estimand is the unweighted mean of paired per-call burden differences across frozen item-by-subject cells. Each cell has equal weight regardless of opportunity count. An observed arm never substitutes for a missing partner. The pooled opportunity-weighted contrast is secondary, must show numerator and denominator, cannot bank a branch, and must be retained when it diverges.

The fixed-seed nonparametric bootstrap samples item identities with replacement inside each frozen family, retaining all subject and arm observations for each sampled item. Subjects are fixed strata, never sampled as a model population. Opportunities are never resampled as independent observations. Any sign/randomization test preserves the same clustering.

Secondary outcomes include component counts; unresolved-residue survival; trap catch; answer utility; inspectability; LANG-A validator profile; decorative compliance; refusal, deletion, truncation, abstention, completeness, over-bounding, positive-conclusion defects; token, latency, and cost; and predeclared family and subject interactions.

## Fixed margins and branch bank

The controlling values are delta 0.10 improvement, epsilon plus or minus 0.05 equivalence, harm 0.10, and family interaction 0.15. Simulation cannot change them.

After integrity and admissibility rules, every predicate is evaluated. Exactly one receipt is selected in order: B-HARM, B-INTERACTION, B-NOTATION, B-SCAFFOLD, B-NULL, otherwise B-INCONCLUSIVE. Secondary predicates and findings remain recorded.

B-NOTATION requires the D_N 95 percent interval wholly at or below negative delta, no frozen harm gate, and core checks. B-SCAFFOLD requires D_S wholly at or below negative delta and D_N wholly within equivalence, with no harm. B-NULL requires all three intervals wholly within equivalence, adequate census, and checks. B-HARM requires a burden interval wholly beyond harm or a frozen anti-taxidermy harm gate. B-INTERACTION requires range greater than 0.15 and two opposite-sign family intervals excluding zero. B-INCONCLUSIVE covers inadequate precision, failed manipulation, inadequate census, unmatched predicates, or limiting lineage/scoring evidence.

Harm has precedence and cannot be hidden by aggregate benefit. Integrity failures remain quarantine/cancellation/inconclusive issues rather than substantive harm.

## Grading, calibration, and adjudication

Scoring has content-normalized and native-artifact views. Two blind first passes cover primary defect categories, with adjudication for disagreement. Calibration uses synthetic permanently tainted examples only. Before locked target scoring, no primary grader may see a target item, packet, rendering or paraphrase, trap class, opportunities, keyed disposition, or target-derived calibration. At the permitted scoring event, the grader receives only the exact source packet corresponding to the locked response; each versioned read is logged. Premature exposure disqualifies that grader from the affected blind panel and retains output as exploratory only.

Calibration requires categorical agreement at least 0.80 and Cohen's kappa at least 0.60 per primary defect family. An owner-frozen replacement rule is required if kappa is undefined. Count disagreement greater than one is adjudicated.

A fresh-chair adjudicator is preferred: no authorship of items, prompts, rendering, rubric, or key; no calibration; no target-output view before locked disagreements; exact adjudication packet only; fresh session; and, where feasible, a different declared family/route or human chair. Fresh session or route does not establish fresh weights or global independence. If no fresh chair exists, shared roots and limitations remain explicit and cannot become independent corroboration.
